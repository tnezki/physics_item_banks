5. Find the acceleration of an object that increases speed from 5.0 m/s to 20 m/s while traveling a distance of 100 m.

8. Calculate the velocity after 2.5 s for an object dropped from rest. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
9. A scientist studying free fall needs to determine how far an object drops. Calculate the distance fallen for a ball dropped from rest that falls for 4.0 s using $g = 9.8$ m/s².
10. Determine the distance an object falls from rest in 3.5 s. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
11. Find the maximum height reached by a ball thrown upward with initial velocity 25 m/s. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
12. An athlete training for a vertical jump competition throws a ball upward to measure performance. Find the maximum height reached when an object is thrown vertically upward at 30 m/s using $g = 9.8$ m/s².
13. A ball thrown upward at 20 m/s takes how long to reach its maximum height? Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
14. Calculate the time to reach maximum height for an object thrown upward at 15 m/s. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
15. A physics student needs to verify a kinematic equation experimentally. Find the final velocity for an object starting at 8.0 m/s that accelerates at 2.5 m/s² and travels 60 m.
16. A car traveling at 20 m/s accelerates at 3.0 m/s² over a distance of 100 m. Calculate the final velocity.
17. Determine the displacement of a train that starts from rest and accelerates at 1.5 m/s² for 20 s.
18. An air traffic controller monitors a plane during takeoff. Find the distance traveled by an airplane that starts from rest and accelerates at 2.0 m/s² for 30 s.
19. An object with initial velocity 10 m/s and final velocity 25 m/s travels for 5.0 s. Calculate the displacement.
20. Find the displacement during acceleration when a car accelerates from 12 m/s to 28 m/s in 8.0 s.
21. A geologist studying cliff erosion drops stones to measure height. Calculate the height from which a stone was dropped if it hits the ground with velocity 35 m/s. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
22. An object in free fall reaches a velocity of 44.1 m/s just before hitting the ground. Determine the height from which it fell. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
23. Calculate the velocity after 2.0 s for a ball thrown downward from a building with initial velocity 8.0 m/s. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
24. A construction worker accidentally drops a tool from a scaffold. Determine the final velocity after 3.0 s for an object thrown downward at 5.0 m/s using $g = 9.8$ m/s².

11. Calculate the time to hit the ground and the final velocity just before impact for a ball dropped from a height of 80 m. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².

17. Calculate the time required and the distance traveled during acceleration for a car that accelerates from rest at 4.0 m/s² until reaching 32 m/s.
18. A search and rescue team needs to estimate impact time and velocity. Calculate the time to reach the ground and the final velocity upon impact for a stone thrown downward at 10 m/s from a cliff 80 m high. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
19. An object thrown upward at 22 m/s takes a certain time to reach peak height. Calculate this time, then determine how high above the starting point the object is after 3.0 s total. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
20. Find the time for deceleration and the displacement during this period when a car traveling at 25 m/s begins decelerating at -4.0 m/s² until reaching 15 m/s.
21. A volleyball player serves a ball upward to practice setting height. Calculate the maximum height above the ground and the total time until the ball hits the ground for a ball thrown upward with velocity 20 m/s from a platform 15 m high. Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².
