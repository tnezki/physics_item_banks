15 questions | Tests understanding of wave concepts | Multiple choice (A-E) | No calculations required
2.1
A wave travels through a rope. The rope particles move up and down while the wave pattern moves horizontally. What type of wave is this, and why?
A A longitudinal wave because particles move parallel to wave direction
B A transverse wave because particles move perpendicular to wave direction
C A transverse wave because it moves horizontally
D A longitudinal wave because it requires a medium
E Cannot be determined without knowing the frequency
2.2
Compare two waves in the same medium: Wave A has a longer wavelength, and Wave B has a shorter wavelength. If both waves were created at the same instant and travel through the same medium, which statement must be true?
A Wave A travels faster than Wave B
B Wave A has a lower frequency than Wave B
C Wave B carries more energy
D Wave A has more crests per meter
E Both waves will eventually become the same
2.3
Light from the sun travels to Earth through the vacuum of space. What does this tell you about whether light is a mechanical or electromagnetic wave?
A Light must be a mechanical wave
B Light must be an electromagnetic wave because it doesn't require a medium
C Light could be either type of wave
D The sun creates a medium for light to travel through
E Light does not actually travel to Earth
2.4
A student creates a wave on a rope by moving one end up and down repeatedly. If the student increases the frequency of oscillation, what happens to the wavelength of the wave on the rope, assuming the rope properties don't change?
A The wavelength increases
B The wavelength stays the same
C The wavelength decreases
D The wavelength becomes zero
E Cannot be determined without knowing the period
2.5
A ripple moves across a pond's surface. An observer floating on a cork notices that the cork bobs up and down but doesn't move across the pond. What does this observation demonstrate?
A Waves transfer matter from place to place
B The cork is too light to move with waves
C Waves transfer energy without transferring matter
D The pond is not deep enough for the wave
E Corks cannot float on waves
2.6
The relationship $f = \frac{1}{T}$ shows that frequency and period are inversely related. If a wave's period increases, what happens to its frequency?
A Frequency increases
B Frequency stays the same
C Frequency decreases
D Frequency becomes zero
E Cannot be determined without knowing wavelength
2.7
Red light and violet light are both electromagnetic waves traveling through space. Violet light has a shorter wavelength than red light. What must be true about their frequencies?
A They have the same frequency
B Red light has higher frequency than violet light
C Violet light has higher frequency than red light
D One wave is transverse and one is longitudinal
E Frequency cannot be compared without knowing wavelength
2.8
In a transverse wave traveling along a rope, at the moment when a point on the rope is at a crest, what can you say about the velocity of that point?
A The point is moving upward at maximum speed
B The point is moving downward at maximum speed
C The point is momentarily at rest (zero velocity)
D The point is moving at the wave speed
E The point is moving horizontally
2.9
Why is it possible for light to reach Earth from distant stars, but sound from those same stars cannot be heard?
A Light travels faster than sound
B Sound waves are too weak to travel far
C Sound is a mechanical wave requiring a medium; space is a vacuum
D The stars are too far away
E Sound waves reflect off space dust
2.10
A spring is stretched horizontally and oscillated back and forth (compressed and stretched). What type of wave is created, and how do the particles move relative to the wave motion?
A Transverse wave; particles move perpendicular to wave propagation
B Longitudinal wave; particles move parallel to wave propagation
C Electromagnetic wave; particles don't move at all
D Transverse wave; particles move parallel to wave propagation
E Cannot determine without knowing the spring constant
2.11
Two wave machines create transverse waves in water. Machine A creates waves at 2 Hz and Machine B creates waves at 4 Hz, both in the same water tank. Assuming water properties don't change, what is the relationship between the wavelengths?
A Both waves have the same wavelength
B Machine A's wavelength is twice Machine B's wavelength
C Machine B's wavelength is twice Machine A's wavelength
D Machine A's wavelength is four times Machine B's wavelength
E Cannot be determined without knowing the wave speed
2.12
A student observes that increasing the tension in a rope causes the waves on that rope to travel faster. If the frequency remains constant, what happens to the wavelength?
A Wavelength decreases
B Wavelength increases
C Wavelength remains constant
D Wavelength becomes zero
E Cannot determine from this information
2.13
Which statement best describes the relationship between wavelength, frequency, and wave speed in any medium?
A Wavelength and frequency are always equal
B Wave speed equals wavelength times frequency
C Frequency determines the wave speed
D These three quantities are unrelated
E Wavelength is always longer than the wave speed
2.14
Electromagnetic waves can travel through vacuum, but they follow the same wave relationship ($v = f\lambda$) as mechanical waves. What does this tell you about wave equations?
A They only work for mechanical waves
B They only work for electromagnetic waves
C They describe fundamental wave properties that apply universally
D Each type of wave requires different equations
E Wave equations are just approximations
2.15
If two waves have different amplitudes but the same frequency and travel in the same medium, how do their wavelengths compare?
A The wave with larger amplitude has longer wavelength
B The wave with smaller amplitude has longer wavelength
C Amplitude does not affect wavelength
D Their wavelengths cannot be compared without knowing speed
E The wavelengths will always be different
