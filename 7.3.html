Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A battery supplies 0.50 A to a circuit for 30 minutes. Calculate the total charge transferred in coulombs.
2. Calculate the average current when a lightning bolt transfers 25 C of charge in 2.0 milliseconds (0.0020 s) in amperes.

Assume $8.5 \times 10^{28}$ free electrons per m³. Use $I = nAve$ where $n$ is electron density, $A$ is area, $v$ is drift velocity, and $e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C. Express answer in m/s.
8. A technician troubleshoots electrical systems by analyzing current patterns. A circuit draws a steady current of 0.25 A. Calculate how long the circuit must run to transfer 0.50 kC (500 C) of charge in seconds.
9. A solar panel produces 0.40 A in bright sunlight. If the panel operates for 6.0 hours, calculate the total charge transferred in coulombs.

11. Calculate the average current when a camera flash capacitor discharges 0.040 C in 0.0010 seconds in amperes.
12. A welding machine draws 150 A. Determine how much charge flows in 2.0 minutes, then calculate how many electrons this represents. (Express electrons in scientific notation.)
13. An electroplating engineer optimizes metal deposition rates. An electroplating process deposits metal using a current of 3.0 A for 45 minutes. Calculate the total charge transferred in coulombs.
14. A battery-powered device draws an average current of 80 mA. Calculate the charge consumed in 8.0 hours in coulombs.
15. A 12-volt car battery delivers 50 A to start a car for 4.0 seconds. Calculate the charge delivered, then determine the energy transferred using $E = qV$ in joules.
16. Calculate the number of electrons that must flow per second to produce a current of 1.0 A. (Express answer in scientific notation.)
17. A circuit requires 500 C of charge over 10 minutes. Determine the average current required in amperes.
18. A researcher analyzing charge transport in nanoscale devices measures electron flow. A nanotechnology device operates with $1.0 \times 10^{10}$ electrons flowing per second. Calculate the current in amperes.
19. Three ammeters are connected in different parts of a series circuit and read 0.50 A, 0.52 A, and 0.49 A due to measurement uncertainty. Calculate the average current in amperes.
20. A capacitor (charge storage device) stores 0.020 C and discharges completely in 0.050 seconds. Calculate the average discharge current in amperes.
21. Determine the time required for $1.0 \times 10^{21}$ electrons to pass through a wire carrying 4.0 A in seconds.
22. An electrical engineer designs power distribution for a facility. A building's electrical system handles an average current of 200 A. Calculate how much charge flows through the main line in 24 hours in coulombs, expressing your answer in scientific notation.
