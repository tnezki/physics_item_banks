Formula 1: Law of Reflection
Formula: $\theta_i = \theta_r$

Variables: $\theta_i$ = angle of incidence (degrees), $\theta_r$ = angle of reflection (degrees)
3.1
A light ray strikes a mirror surface at an angle of 35° from the normal line. Calculate the angle of reflection.
Answer: _____ degrees
3.2
Find the angle of incidence if a sound wave reflects off a wall at an angle of 52° from the normal.
Answer: _____ degrees
3.3
A laser beam approaches a reflective surface with an angle of incidence of 28° from the normal. What is the angle at which the reflected beam leaves the surface?
Answer: _____ degrees
3.4
A ray of light hits a smooth mirror at 68° from the normal line. Determine the angle of reflection.
Answer: _____ degrees
Formula 2: Snell's Law
Formula: $n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$

Variables: $n$ = index of refraction (unitless), $\theta$ = angle from normal (degrees)
3.5
Light travels from air ($n_1 = 1.00$) into water ($n_2 = 1.33$) at an angle of incidence of 45°. Calculate the angle of refraction in the water.
Answer: _____ degrees
3.6
Find the angle of incidence when light enters glass ($n_2 = 1.52$) from air ($n_1 = 1.00$) and refracts to an angle of 25° in the glass.
Answer: _____ degrees
3.7
A light wave travels from water ($n_1 = 1.33$) into air ($n_2 = 1.00$) at an angle of incidence of 30°. Determine the angle of refraction in air.
Answer: _____ degrees
3.8
Light enters a diamond ($n_2 = 2.42$) from air ($n_1 = 1.00$) at an incident angle of 30°. Calculate the refraction angle inside the diamond.
Answer: _____ degrees
Formula 3: Constructive Interference
Formula: Path difference = $n\lambda$

Variables: $n$ = order number (0, 1, 2...), $\lambda$ = wavelength
3.9
Two light waves with a wavelength of 550 nm undergo constructive interference at order $n = 2$. Calculate the path difference between the two waves.
Answer: _____ nm
3.10
Find the wavelength if sound waves constructively interfere with a path difference of 3.0 m at order $n = 5$.
Answer: _____ m
3.11
Two speakers produce waves with a wavelength of 0.4 m. At a certain point, constructive interference occurs at order $n = 3$. What is the path difference between the waves?
Answer: _____ m
3.12
Light with wavelength 450 nm creates constructive interference at the first bright fringe ($n = 1$). Calculate the path difference at this location.
Answer: _____ nm
Formula 4: Destructive Interference
Formula: Path difference = $(n + \frac{1}{2})\lambda$

Variables: $n$ = order number (0, 1, 2...), $\lambda$ = wavelength
3.13
Two light waves with wavelength 600 nm undergo destructive interference at order $n = 1$. Calculate the path difference.
Answer: _____ nm
3.14
Find the wavelength if sound waves destructively interfere with a path difference of 2.25 m at order $n = 2$.
Answer: _____ m
3.15
Two speakers produce waves with wavelength 0.5 m. Destructive interference occurs at order $n = 0$. What is the path difference?
Answer: _____ m
3.16
Light with wavelength 500 nm creates a dark fringe (destructive interference) at order $n = 3$. Calculate the path difference at this dark fringe.
Answer: _____ nm
