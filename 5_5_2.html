C The stethoscope creates new sound waves

B The wavelength decreases, frequency increases, and you hear a higher pitch

D The speed of sound increases
E The timbre of the siren changes

C Radar cannot actually measure speed; only speed guns can
D The wavelength of radar waves changes with car color

B The vibration frequency increases, producing a higher pitch

E The timbre becomes less complex

D 110 dB is impossible to reach at a concert

B Light travels much faster than sound; sound takes significant time to travel large distances
C Sound and light travel at the same speed
D The accident produces light but not sound
E Distance affects light speed but not sound speed

A High frequency sounds are louder
B High frequencies produce shorter wavelengths; smaller wavelengths can reflect from smaller objects
C Ultrasound reflects off any object, regardless of size
D Insects cannot hear 50 kHz sounds
E The prey's echolocation interferes with the bat's calls

A The Doppler effect
B Refraction of sound waves
C Interference: constructive interference (loud) and destructive interference (quiet) as waves from both forks overlap
D Diffraction around obstacles
E Absorption by the air molecules

B Harmonics produce the loudness of the sound

D Harmonics prevent the string from vibrating

A The flute is louder
B The saxophone has higher pitch
C Different harmonic combinations (timbre) create different tone qualities at the same pitch
D Sound waves travel differently through wood and metal
E The frequency of the note changes depending on the instrument

B The sound traveled down and back up; the total distance is 2 seconds × sound speed, so depth is half that distance
C The depth equals 2 seconds × sound speed
D The depth cannot be determined without knowing the ship's position
E Sonar measures depth by measuring reflected light, not sound

A 90 dB is louder than a jet engine
B Damage is cumulative; prolonged exposure above 85 dB accumulates over time and causes permanent hearing loss
C Hearing protection only works for sounds above 100 dB

E Hair cells in the ear regenerate, so damage is reversible

B The diaphragm's back-and-forth motion at 100 Hz produces compressions and rarefactions at 100 Hz
C The diaphragm produces compressions but not rarefactions
D The diaphragm's vibration frequency is unrelated to the sound frequency
E The diaphragm produces only rarefactions

A Sound travels faster than light in water
B Light is absorbed quickly in dark water, but sound can travel long distances and return as echoes
C Dolphins have better hearing than vision
D Echolocation does not work in water
E Only dolphins can produce sound underwater

B Matching frequency ensures all instruments have the same loudness
C The frequency of the tuning note determines the timbre of all instruments
D Frequency matching prevents sound from traveling to the audience

D Distant lightning produces less energy and slower sound
