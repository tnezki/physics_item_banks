Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A bat emits an ultrasonic pulse at 40,000 Hz. Sound travels at 343 m/s in air. Calculate the wavelength, then determine how many wavelengths are in a 10 m distance.
2. An observer sees a firework explode and hears the sound 2.0 seconds later. Calculate the distance using sound speed 343 m/s. Then determine how long light took to reach you (light speed = $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s).
3. A train whistle operating at 500 Hz approaches a stationary observer at 30 m/s. Sound speed is 340 m/s. Calculate the observed frequency using $f' = f\frac{v}{v - v_s}$. Then calculate the wavelength of the sound perceived by the observer.
4. At 0°C, sound travels at 331 m/s through air. Calculate the wavelength of middle C (262 Hz) at this temperature. Then determine what the wavelength becomes at 30°C where sound travels at 349 m/s.
5. A physics demonstration uses a sound wave with wavelength 0.50 m in air at 20°C where v = 343 m/s. Calculate the frequency. This wave then enters water where sound travels at 1500 m/s. Determine the new wavelength in water (frequency unchanged).
6. Two tuning forks produce sounds at 440 Hz and 445 Hz. When sounded together, they create audible beats. Calculate the beat frequency (difference). Then determine the period of one beat.
7. A fire truck with a 1000 Hz siren moves away from you at 25 m/s. Sound speed is 340 m/s. Calculate the observed frequency using $f' = f\frac{v}{v + v_s}$. Then find the wavelength you observe.
8. You shout toward a cliff and hear an echo 2.0 seconds later. Sound travels at 343 m/s. Calculate the total distance sound traveled (to cliff and back). Then determine the distance to the cliff (half the total).
9. A dolphin uses echolocation with 100 kHz ($1.0 \times 10^5$ Hz) ultrasound in water where sound travels at 1500 m/s. Calculate the wavelength. Then determine how long it takes for the sound to travel 300 m to a fish and back.
10. An atmospheric scientist measures how sound speed changes with temperature. Calculate the speed at 10°C and at 30°C using $v = 331 + 0.6T$. Then verify that the difference equals 12 m/s.
11. A sound source moving toward you at 20 m/s emits 600 Hz. Sound speed is 340 m/s. Calculate the observed frequency using $f' = f\frac{v}{v - v_s}$. Then calculate the wavelength of what you hear.
12. You stand between two parallel canyon walls 680 m apart and shout. Sound travels at 340 m/s. Calculate the time for sound to reach the nearest wall (340 m away) and return. Then calculate the time for sound to reach the far wall and return.
13. A submarine's sonar detects a reflection 0.80 s after emitting a pulse. Sound travels at 1500 m/s in seawater. Calculate the total distance traveled. Then determine the distance to the reflecting object.
14. An acoustics lab measures a musical note with wavelength 0.78 m in air traveling at 340 m/s. Calculate its frequency. Then calculate the ratio of this frequency to A440 to identify the note.
15. An ambulance approaches you at 30 m/s with its 800 Hz siren blaring. Sound speed is 340 m/s. Calculate the frequency you hear as it approaches using $f' = f\frac{v}{v - v_s}$. Then calculate the frequency as it recedes using $f' = f\frac{v}{v + v_s}$.
16. Medical imaging requires high-resolution ultrasound at 3.0 MHz ($3.0 \times 10^6$ Hz) in tissue where sound travels at 1540 m/s. Calculate the wavelength. Then determine how many complete wavelengths fit in 3.0 cm of tissue.
17. Two musicians playing together notice an unpleasant warbling sound. Their instruments produce 256 Hz and 260 Hz. Calculate the beat frequency. Then determine how many times per second the loudness peaks occur.
18. A geology student investigates acoustics in a canyon. The canyon wall is 850 m away, and you clap your hands. Calculate the time for the echo to return using sound speed 340 m/s. Then determine what frequency sound would have wavelength equal to the canyon width.
19. A race car approaches at 60 m/s producing engine sound at 400 Hz. Sound speed is 340 m/s. Calculate the Doppler-shifted frequency you hear as it approaches. Then determine what you hear as it recedes at the same speed.
20. An engineer tests sound transmission through a 20 m steel beam. Sound travels at 5000 m/s in steel. A 500 Hz sound travels through the beam. Calculate the wavelength in steel. Then determine how many complete wavelengths fit in the beam.
