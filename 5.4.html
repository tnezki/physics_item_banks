1. A 2.0 kg cart moving at 3.0 m/s collides with a 1.0 kg cart initially at rest. If the carts stick together, determine the final velocity of the system.
2. Determine the final velocity when a 0.40 kg ball moving at 5.0 m/s collides with a stationary 0.60 kg ball and the two stick together.
3. Two carts on a frictionless track collide and lock together. Cart A has a mass of 1.5 kg and moves at 4.0 m/s, while Cart B has a mass of 2.5 kg and is initially at rest. Calculate the final velocity.
4. Calculate the speed after collision when a 1,200 kg car traveling at 18 m/s collides with a stopped 800 kg car and the vehicles stick together.
5. A 0.25 kg puck slides east at 12 m/s and collides with a 0.50 kg puck at rest. Determine the final velocity if the pucks stick together.
6. Find the final speed of the system when a 900 kg motorcycle moving at 25 m/s collides with a 1,100 kg car initially at rest and the two move together.
7. Two skaters push off from rest. One skater has a mass of 60 kg and moves away at 2.5 m/s. Determine the speed of the second skater if their mass is 75 kg.
8. Determine the final velocity after a 3.0 kg cart moving east at 4.0 m/s collides with a 1.5 kg cart moving east at 1.0 m/s and the carts stick together.
9. A 20,000 kg train car rolling at 2.0 m/s couples with a stationary 15,000 kg car. Calculate the final speed after coupling.
10. Find the final velocity of the system when a 2.0 kg object sliding east at 5.0 m/s collides with a 1.0 kg object sliding west at 3.0 m/s and the objects stick together.
11. A 1.2 kg ball moving at 7.0 m/s collides with a stationary 0.80 kg ball. Calculate the final velocity if the balls stick together.
12. A runner jumps from a movable platform. If a 70 kg runner lands with a horizontal speed of 4.0 m/s and the platform has a mass of 350 kg, determine the platform’s speed after the jump.
13. A 0.30 kg ball moves at 10 m/s and collides with a 0.90 kg ball initially at rest. Determine the final velocity if the balls stick together.
14. Determine the final speed when a 1,000 kg car moving at 12 m/s collides with a 500 kg car traveling at 4.0 m/s in the same direction and the cars lock together.
15. Two carts collide and stick together. Cart A has a mass of 1.0 kg and moves at 5.0 m/s, while Cart B has a mass of 4.0 kg and is initially at rest. Calculate the final velocity.
16. Two identical objects collide. If a 0.50 kg object moving at 8.0 m/s collides with another identical object at rest and the objects stick together, find the final velocity.
17. A person of mass 65 kg pushes a 35 kg crate initially at rest. After the push, the person moves backward at 1.5 m/s. Determine the speed of the crate.
18. Calculate the final velocity when a 2.5 kg object traveling at 6.0 m/s collides with a stationary 2.5 kg object and the two stick together.
19. A 1,200 kg car moving at 15 m/s collides with a 1,200 kg car traveling at 5.0 m/s in the opposite direction. Determine the final velocity if the cars lock together.
20. Two students on roller skates push off from rest. One student (50 kg) moves backward at 2.0 m/s. Determine the speed of the second student if their mass is 65 kg.

Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response, HARD)

1. Determine the final velocity of the system when a 60 kg skateboarder moving at 4.0 m/s jumps off a skateboard that continues forward at 2.0 m/s. The skateboard has a mass of 3.0 kg.
2. Two ice skaters push off from rest on frictionless ice. One skater has a mass of 50 kg and moves away at 3.0 m/s. Calculate the speed of the second skater, whose mass is 70 kg.
3. A 0.20 kg hockey puck sliding east at 12 m/s collides with a stationary puck of mass 0.30 kg. Find the final velocity if the pucks stick together after the collision.
4. Find the common velocity of the two vehicles when a 1,200 kg car traveling at 18 m/s rear-ends a stopped 900 kg car and the cars lock together.
5. A baseball is thrown horizontally at 40 m/s and is caught by a glove, coming to rest. Calculate the change in momentum of the 0.15 kg baseball.
6. Determine the person’s speed after the throw when a 75 kg person standing on a frictionless surface throws a 2.0 kg ball forward at 10 m/s.
7. Two carts move toward each other on a track. Cart A has a mass of 2.0 kg and moves at 3.0 m/s to the right, while Cart B has a mass of 1.5 kg and moves at 4.0 m/s to the left. Calculate the total momentum of the system before the collision.
8. Two carts move toward each other on a track. Cart A has a mass of 2.0 kg and moves at 3.0 m/s to the right, while Cart B has a mass of 1.5 kg and moves at 4.0 m/s to the left. After the collision, the carts stick together. Calculate the velocity of the combined carts.
9. Determine the final speed when a 900 kg motorcycle and rider moving at 25 m/s collide with a 1,100 kg car moving at 10 m/s in the same direction and the vehicles lock together.
10. Calculate the recoil speed of the cannon when a cannon of mass 800 kg fires a 5.0 kg projectile at 120 m/s.
11. A 0.50 kg ball moving at 6.0 m/s collides head-on with an identical ball moving at 2.0 m/s in the opposite direction. Determine their final velocity if the balls stick together.
12. Find the platform’s speed after the jump when a 70 kg runner jumps forward off a stationary platform and lands with a horizontal speed of 4.0 m/s. The platform has a mass of 350 kg and is free to roll.
13. Two carts collide and stick together. Cart A has a mass of 1.0 kg and a velocity of 5.0 m/s, while Cart B has a mass of 3.0 kg and is initially at rest. Determine the final velocity of the combined system.
14. A tennis ball strikes a wall at 20 m/s and rebounds straight back at 18 m/s. Calculate the change in momentum of the 0.10 kg tennis ball.
15. Determine the final speed after coupling when a 20,000 kg freight train car rolling at 2.0 m/s couples with a stationary car of mass 15,000 kg.
16. A 2.0 kg object sliding east at 6.0 m/s collides with a 1.0 kg object sliding west at 4.0 m/s. Calculate the final velocity of the system if the objects stick together.
17. Find the speed of the crate after the push when a 65 kg person pushes a 35 kg crate initially at rest and then moves backward at 1.5 m/s.
18. A 0.25 kg puck moves at 14 m/s and collides with a 0.50 kg puck at rest. Determine their final velocity if the pucks stick together.
19. Explain quantitatively why the rocket moves forward by calculating the rocket’s momentum change when 2.0 kg of gas is expelled backward at 500 m/s.
20. Two students on roller skates push off from rest. One student has a mass of 45 kg and moves backward at 2.5 m/s. Calculate the speed of the second student, whose mass is 60 kg.
