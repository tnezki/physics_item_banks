Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A 2200 μF capacitor is charged to 12 volts. Calculate: (a) charge stored, (b) energy stored. Answer: energy in joules.
2. A camera flash capacitor stores 10 J of energy. If its capacitance is 1000 μF, determine the charging voltage. Answer in volts.
3. A circuit has a 100 kΩ resistor in series with a 220 μF capacitor. Calculate the time constant. Then determine how many seconds until the capacitor is about 63% charged (which equals one time constant). Answer: time in seconds.
4. Two capacitors of 100 μF each are in parallel. Calculate the total capacitance, then determine the charge when charged to 9.0 volts. Answer: charge in coulombs.
5. A 500 μF capacitor charged to 20V discharges through a 10 kΩ resistor. Calculate: (a) initial energy, (b) time constant. Answer: time constant in seconds.
6. Calculate the energy stored in a 3300 μF capacitor at 15 volts, then determine how much power would be delivered if this energy is released in 0.01 seconds. Answer: power in watts.
7. A power supply uses a 4700 μF capacitor charged to 25 volts. Calculate stored energy, then determine the charge. Answer: charge in coulombs.
8. Two capacitors are in series: 200 μF and 300 μF. For series capacitors, $1/C_{total} = 1/C_1 + 1/C_2$. Calculate total capacitance, then find charge when charged to 12 volts. Answer: charge in coulombs.
9. A 1000 μF capacitor stores 0.18 J. Calculate the voltage, then determine what charge is stored. Answer: charge in coulombs.
10. An RC circuit has $R = 22$ kΩ and needs a time constant of 2.0 seconds. Calculate the required capacitance. Answer in microfarads.
11. A solar energy system uses a capacitor bank with 10,000 μF total capacitance at 400 volts. Calculate the stored energy. Answer in kilojoules.
12. A 680 μF capacitor at 18 volts discharges to 9.0 volts (half voltage). Calculate: (a) initial energy, (b) final energy, (c) energy released. Answer: energy released in joules.
13. Three identical capacitors of 150 μF each are in parallel. Calculate total capacitance, then find total charge at 20 volts. Answer: charge in coulombs.
14. A touchscreen sensor has 50 pF capacitance. When touched, it changes to 60 pF. At 3.3 volts, calculate the change in stored charge. Answer in picocoulombs (pC).
15. A defibrillator capacitor of 32 μF is charged to 5000 volts. Calculate the stored energy. Answer in joules.
16. An electric vehicle uses capacitors for regenerative braking. A 10 F (yes, 10 full farads) capacitor is charged to 48 volts. Calculate stored energy. Answer in kilojoules.
17. A 1500 μF capacitor is charged by a constant 2.0 mA current for 10 seconds. Calculate the charge accumulated, then the final voltage. Answer: voltage in volts.
18. Two capacitors in parallel: 470 μF and 1000 μF. Calculate total capacitance. At 12 volts, calculate total stored energy. Answer: energy in joules.
19. A flash charges a 2000 μF capacitor to 300 volts in 5.0 seconds. Calculate stored energy, then average charging power. Answer: power in watts.
20. A circuit has two capacitors in series: 100 μF and 200 μF (use reciprocal formula). Calculate equivalent capacitance, then find energy stored at 30 volts. Answer: energy in joules.
