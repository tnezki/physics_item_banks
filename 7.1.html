Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. Three charges are arranged along a line: $+2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at position $x = 0$ m, $+4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at $x = 0.30$ m, and $-3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at $x = 0.60$ m. Calculate the net force on the middle charge in newtons. (Forces from the left and right charges act in opposite directions.)
2. Two point charges of $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and $-3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are initially 0.40 m apart. If the distance is reduced to 0.20 m, calculate how many times stronger the force becomes.
3. A physics student designs a demonstration with charged spheres. A $+6.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge experiences a force of 0.80 N from another charge 0.25 m away. Calculate the magnitude of the second charge in coulombs.
4. Two charges exert a force of 2.0 N on each other when separated by 0.30 m. Calculate the force in newtons when the separation is increased to 0.60 m.
5. A charge of $+4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and an unknown charge are 0.15 m apart and exert a force of 1.2 N on each other. Determine the magnitude of the unknown charge in coulombs.
6. Three identical charges of $+2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are placed at the corners of an equilateral triangle with sides 0.20 m long. Calculate the net force on one charge due to the other two in newtons. (Hint: Use vector addition and trigonometry.)
7. Determine the distance between two charges when a $+3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge and a $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge exert a force of 0.50 N on each other in meters.
8. An engineer calibrating sensors measures forces between test charges. Two charges of $+8.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and $-2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are separated by distance $r$ and exert a force of 0.90 N. Calculate the separation distance in meters.
9. A charge of $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C is fixed at the origin. Another charge of $-3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C is placed 0.40 m to the right. A third charge of $+2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C is placed 0.20 m to the left of the origin. Calculate the net force on the charge at the origin in newtons.
10. Two charges exert a force $F$ on each other. If one charge is tripled and the distance is halved, calculate how many times larger the new force is compared to the original.
11. Calculate the ratio of electric force to gravitational force between two protons separated by $1.0 \times 10^{-10}$ m. Each proton has charge $+1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C and mass $1.67 \times 10^{-27}$ kg. Use $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}$ N·m²/kg².
12. A researcher studying particle interactions positions charges along a line. Charges of $+4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C, $-6.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C, and $+3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are positioned at $x = 0$ m, $x = 0.50$ m, and $x = 1.0$ m respectively. Calculate the net force on the charge at $x = 0.50$ m in newtons.
13. Two charges are initially separated by 0.25 m and exert a force of 1.8 N on each other. Determine the new separation distance in meters if the force increases to 7.2 N.
14. A charge of $+7.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C is placed at the origin. Two charges of $-2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are placed at positions $(0.30, 0)$ m and $(0, 0.40)$ m. Calculate the magnitude of the net force on the positive charge in newtons.
15. Calculate the magnitude of charge needed so that two identical charges separated by 0.50 m exert a force of 0.36 N on each other in coulombs.
16. Four charges are arranged at the corners of a square with sides 0.10 m. Three corners have $+3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and one corner has $-3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C. Calculate the net force on the negative charge in newtons.
17. Determine how much the distance must change if doubling both charges results in the force remaining the same. Express your answer as a multiple of the original distance.
18. A technician testing electrostatic equipment places a $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge at the origin. Calculate the position on the x-axis (in meters) where a $-2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge should be placed so it experiences a force of 0.45 N.
19. Three charges lie on a straight line: $+Q$ at $x = 0$ m, $+2Q$ at $x = 0.40$ m, and $-3Q$ at $x = 0.80$ m. If $Q = 2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C, calculate the net force on the charge at $x = 0.40$ m in newtons.
20. Two charges of $+6.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and $+3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are fixed 0.60 m apart. Calculate where along the line between them (distance from the $+6.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge in meters) a third charge of $+1.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C experiences zero net force.
21. A charge experiences a force of 1.5 N when placed 0.20 m from a $+8.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge. If this charge is moved to 0.35 m from the same source charge, calculate the new force in newtons.
22. An electrical engineer optimizes component spacing on a circuit board. Two components with charges $+4.5 \times 10^{-6}$ C and $-7.5 \times 10^{-6}$ C exert a force of 2.7 N on each other. Calculate their separation distance in meters.
