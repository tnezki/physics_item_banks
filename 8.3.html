Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A series circuit has three resistors: 10 ohms, 15 ohms, and 25 ohms connected to a 60-volt battery. First calculate the total resistance, then determine the current flowing through the circuit. Answer: current in amperes.
2. Three resistors of 5.0 ohms, 10 ohms, and 15 ohms are in series with a battery. The current is 2.0 amperes. First find the total resistance, then calculate the battery voltage. Answer: voltage in volts.
3. A series circuit has resistors of 20 ohms and 40 ohms with a 90-volt battery. Calculate the current, then determine the voltage drop across the 40-ohm resistor. Answer: voltage across 40Ω in volts.
4. Two branches in parallel have resistances of 12 ohms and 24 ohms. First calculate the equivalent resistance, then determine the total current when connected to a 36-volt battery. Answer: total current in amperes.
5. A parallel circuit has a 48-volt battery with two branches: 16 ohms and 24 ohms. Calculate the current in each branch, then find the total current. Answer: total current in amperes.
6. A series circuit has a 12-volt battery and resistors of 8.0 ohms and 16 ohms. Find the current, then calculate the power dissipated by the 16-ohm resistor. Answer: power in watts.
7. Three identical 60-ohm resistors are connected in series to a 180-volt source. Calculate the current, then determine the voltage across each resistor. Answer: voltage across one resistor in volts.
8. Three identical 60-ohm resistors are connected in parallel to a 180-volt source. Calculate the equivalent resistance, then determine the total current. Answer: total current in amperes.
9. A technician has three 30-ohm resistors and needs 10 ohms total resistance. Determine if this is possible using parallel connection. If yes, calculate the total resistance. Answer: total resistance in ohms.
10. A circuit has a 24-volt battery in series with resistors of 4.0 ohms, 6.0 ohms, and 10 ohms. Calculate the current, then determine what percentage of the total voltage drops across the 10-ohm resistor. Answer: percentage.
11. A parallel circuit has three branches: 10 ohms, 20 ohms, and 30 ohms, all connected to a 60-volt battery. Calculate the current in the 10-ohm branch, then the current in the 30-ohm branch. Answer: current in 30Ω branch in amperes.
12. Two resistors in series (30 ohms and 70 ohms) are connected to a 200-volt source. Calculate the current, then find the voltage across the 30-ohm resistor. Answer: voltage in volts.
13. An electrician connects two 20-ohm resistors in parallel, then connects this combination in series with a 10-ohm resistor. All are powered by a 60-volt battery. First find the equivalent resistance of the parallel pair, then the total circuit resistance, then the current. Answer: circuit current in amperes.
14. A series circuit has four identical resistors and a total resistance of 120 ohms. Determine the resistance of each individual resistor. Then, if the same four resistors were connected in parallel, calculate the new total resistance. Answer: parallel total resistance in ohms.
15. A home circuit has five 100-watt bulbs in parallel at 120 volts. First calculate the resistance of one bulb, then determine the total current when all five are on. Answer: total current in amperes.
16. A battery delivers 2.0 amperes to a series circuit containing three resistors: 5.0 ohms, 10 ohms, and 15 ohms. Calculate the total resistance, then determine the battery voltage. Then find the power supplied by the battery. Answer: power in watts.
17. Two parallel branches have 15 ohms and 30 ohms. First find the equivalent resistance. If this parallel combination is then placed in series with a 10-ohm resistor and powered by a 40-volt battery, determine the total circuit current. Answer: current in amperes.
18. A series circuit has resistors of 12 ohms, 18 ohms, and 30 ohms. When connected to a battery, the voltage across the 18-ohm resistor is measured as 36 volts. Calculate the current, then determine the battery voltage. Answer: battery voltage in volts.
19. A parallel circuit at 24 volts has four identical resistors. The total current is 8.0 amperes. Calculate the total resistance, then determine each individual resistor's resistance. Answer: individual resistance in ohms.
20. An engineer needs 7.5 ohms total resistance from two resistors. One resistor is 30 ohms. If they are connected in parallel, calculate what the second resistor must be. Answer: second resistor in ohms.
21. A battery supplies 3.0 amperes to a parallel circuit with three branches: 20 ohms, 30 ohms, and 60 ohms. Calculate the equivalent resistance, then determine the battery voltage. Answer: voltage in volts.
22. A series circuit has five identical resistors and draws 0.40 amperes from a 20-volt battery. Calculate the total resistance, then determine each resistor's value. Answer: individual resistance in ohms.
