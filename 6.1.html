Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. Two plane mirrors are placed at 90° to each other. A light ray strikes the first mirror at an angle of incidence of 40°. After reflecting from the first mirror, the ray strikes the second mirror. Calculate the angle of incidence on the second mirror in degrees.
2. Calculate the angle of incidence on a second parallel mirror when a light ray reflects off a plane mirror at 35° and then strikes the second mirror.
3. A person standing in front of a mirror needs to know how far away their image appears. You stand 1.8 m in front of a plane mirror. Calculate the total distance between you and your image in meters.
4. A person stands 2.5 m from a plane mirror. How far must the light travel from the person to the mirror and back to their eyes in meters?
5. An optical engineer is designing a periscope using two mirrors positioned at an angle of 60° to each other. A light ray strikes the first mirror at an angle of incidence of 30° and reflects to the second mirror. Calculate the angle of incidence on the second mirror in degrees.
6. Determine the angle through which a laser beam's direction has changed after it reflects off a plane mirror, given that the incident beam makes an angle of 25° with the mirror surface itself (not the normal).
7. Three mirrors are arranged in an equilateral triangle. A light ray enters and makes an angle of incidence of 20° on the first mirror. After reflecting from all three mirrors and returning toward its source, calculate the total angle of incidence on the third mirror before exiting, in degrees.
8. A light ray strikes a mirror at an angle of 50° to the normal, reflects, and travels 3.0 m before striking a parallel mirror. If the mirrors are separated by 1.5 m, calculate the angle of incidence on the second mirror in degrees.
9. A submarine periscope designer needs to calculate the vertical height change in a periscope design. A periscope uses two parallel plane mirrors separated by 80 cm, with light entering at an angle of 45° to the normal on the first mirror. Calculate the vertical height difference between where light enters and exits in centimeters.
10. Two plane mirrors form a V-shape with an angle of 45° between them. A horizontal light ray strikes the first mirror. After reflecting from both mirrors, calculate the angle of incidence on the second mirror in degrees.
11. Calculate the total time for light's journey when a light ray traveling at $3.00 \times 10^8$ m/s strikes a mirror at an angle of incidence of 32°, reflects, and travels 4.5 m before and 4.5 m after reflection, in nanoseconds (ns).
12. A laser beam reflects off two perpendicular mirrors, striking the first mirror at 28° to the normal. After two reflections, calculate the total angle through which the original beam direction has changed in degrees.
13. An interior designer is placing objects and people in a room with a mirror. You place a small object 0.75 m in front of a plane mirror while standing 2.00 m in front of the same mirror. Calculate the distance from your eyes to the image of the object in meters.
14. Determine the angle of incidence on a second parallel mirror 3.0 m away when a light ray makes an angle of 15° with the surface of a plane mirror, reflects, and then travels 6.0 m before hitting the second mirror.
15. Two mirrors are placed at 120° to each other. A light ray strikes the first mirror at an angle of incidence of 35°. After reflecting to the second mirror, calculate the angle of incidence on the second mirror in degrees.
16. A satellite engineer tests a corner reflector made of three mutually perpendicular plane mirrors. A light ray enters with an angle of incidence of 25° on the first mirror. After three reflections (one off each mirror), calculate the angle of incidence on the third mirror in degrees.
17. Two parallel mirrors face each other, separated by 1.2 m. A light ray enters at an angle of incidence of 40° on the first mirror. After reflecting back and forth twice (hitting each mirror twice), calculate the total distance the light travels in meters before exiting.
18. Calculate the time required for light to travel 12 m through air after it strikes a mirror at 65° to the normal and reflects. Use $c = 3.00 \times 10^8$ m/s and express your answer in nanoseconds (ns).
19. An interior designer is working on a room design with parallel mirror walls 4.0 m apart. You stand 1.5 m from one mirror. Calculate the distance to your image in the opposite mirror (considering just the first reflection from the far mirror) in meters.
20. A light ray enters a system of three mirrors arranged at 90° angles to each other. The incident angle on the first mirror is 18°. After reflecting from all three mirrors sequentially, calculate the angle of incidence on the third mirror in degrees.
21. Determine the angle of incidence when the reflected ray hits the second mirror, given that two mirrors meet at an angle of 30° to each other and a light ray strikes the first mirror at an angle of incidence of 40°.
22. A technician is calibrating a laser system. A laser beam travels 8.5 m through air to a mirror at an angle of incidence of 38°, reflects, and travels another 8.5 m. Calculate the total time for the light's journey in nanoseconds (ns) using $c = 3.00 \times 10^8$ m/s.
