A. always equal to the object's weight

A. newtons

A. kinetic friction
B. static friction

A. suddenly decreases because kinetic friction is typically less than static friction

A. changing the object's mass

A. accelerate faster

Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A 50 kg box is placed on a horizontal surface. With $\mu_s = 0.40$ and $\mu_k = 0.25$, calculate the maximum static friction using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Then calculate the kinetic friction if the box is pushed and starts sliding.
2. During a laboratory investigation, an 85 kg object rests on a floor. Determine the normal force using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. With $\mu_k = 0.18$, calculate the kinetic friction force when the object slides. Finally, find the net force if an applied force of 200 N pushes the object.
3. A 45 kg child's wagon is being pulled across a surface. The normal force is 350 N. Calculate the maximum static friction where $\mu_s = 0.35$. Then determine the kinetic friction where $\mu_k = 0.20$. What force is required to overcome static friction and start the wagon moving?
4. For safety purposes, a 75 kg person is being tested on a carpeted floor. Calculate the person's weight on Earth using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Then calculate the maximum static friction where $\mu_s = 0.60$. What force is required to make the person start sliding?
5. A warehouse worker needs to move a 120 kg box on a floor where $\mu_s = 0.35$ and $\mu_k = 0.22$. Determine the normal force using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Calculate both the maximum static friction and the kinetic friction. If the box is pushed with 600 N, find the net force and the resulting acceleration.
6. A physics student investigates friction on different surfaces. A 30 kg object is pushed where $\mu_s = 0.50$. Using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, calculate the maximum static friction force. After the object starts moving with $\mu_k = 0.30$, calculate the kinetic friction. How much less force is needed to keep the object moving compared to starting it?
7. A 65 kg crate is being moved across a horizontal floor with $\mu_k = 0.25$. Using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, calculate the normal force. Then find the friction force. If an applied force of 250 N pushes the crate, calculate the net force and the resulting acceleration.
8. An 80 kg object is on an incline where the normal force is 650 N and the component of weight along the incline is 520 N. With $\mu_k = 0.15$, calculate the friction force. Determine whether the object will slide down by comparing to maximum static friction where $\mu_s = 0.80$.
9. Two identical surfaces have different friction coefficients for comparison. Surface A has $\mu_k = 0.20$ and surface B has $\mu_k = 0.40$. A 50 kg box slides on each surface using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Calculate the friction force on each surface and find the difference.
10. A tall building has carpeted elevator floors with specific friction properties. A 95 kg person is in an elevator accelerating upward at $2.0 \text{ m/s}^2$ on Earth where $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Calculate the person's apparent weight (normal force). With $\mu_s = 0.65$, calculate the maximum static friction. Could the person remain stationary if the elevator suddenly decelerated at $3.0 \text{ m/s}^2$?
11. A 55 kg object is on a surface where $\mu_s = 0.45$ and $\mu_k = 0.28$. Determine the normal force using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Find both the maximum static friction and kinetic friction. If a 300 N force is applied horizontally, determine whether the object will move and calculate the acceleration if it does.
12. A delivery service is testing friction on different floor types. A 40 kg box is dragged across a floor with $\mu_k = 0.32$ by a rope held at 30° above horizontal. The tension in the rope is 180 N. Calculate the normal force considering the upward component of rope tension. Then find the friction force and the net horizontal force.
13. A 70 kg sled is being pulled across snow where $\mu_s = 0.30$ and $\mu_k = 0.15$. Using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, calculate the maximum static friction. Once moving, find the kinetic friction. If the sled is pulled with a force of 250 N, calculate the resulting acceleration.
14. During a mechanical systems analysis, a 60 kg block is on a horizontal surface with $\mu_s = 0.55$ and $\mu_k = 0.35$. Using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, calculate the maximum static friction force. Then determine what happens if a 400 N force is applied: find the kinetic friction, the net force, and the acceleration.
15. Two identical blocks are tested on very different surfaces to compare friction effects. One 50 kg block is on an icy surface with $\mu_k = 0.15$ and another is on a rough surface with $\mu_k = 0.60$. Each is pushed with the same 200 N force using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Calculate the friction on each surface, the net force on each block, and the acceleration of each block.
