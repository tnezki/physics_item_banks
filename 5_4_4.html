Scenario 1: Reflection Geometry and Analysis
4.1
A light ray strikes a plane mirror at an angle of 25° from the normal. The reflected ray travels 1.5 m horizontally before hitting a second mirror at 45° to the first. Calculate the angle of reflection at the second mirror using the law of reflection and geometry principles.
Answer: _____ degrees
4.2
A sound wave reflects off a curved mirror. The incident angle is 40° from the normal, and the wave travels through the air at 340 m/s. If the reflected wave must travel 5.1 m to reach a receiver, how much time elapses from reflection until the receiver detects the sound? (Assume the angle of reflection equals the angle of incidence.)
Answer: _____ milliseconds
4.3
An engineer designs a reflective surface where the angle of incidence is 55° from the normal. The incident beam has an intensity of 100 W/m². If 92% of the energy reflects and 8% is absorbed, calculate the reflected power per unit area hitting a target 2.0 m away that subtends an angle based on the reflection geometry.
Answer: _____ W/m²
4.4
A periscope uses two plane mirrors each at 45° angles. A light ray enters the first mirror at an incident angle of 45°. Using the law of reflection twice, and considering the geometry of the mirror arrangement, calculate the total angular deviation of the light ray after passing through both mirrors.
Answer: _____ degrees
Scenario 2: Refraction and Light Transmission
4.5
Light enters water from air at an incident angle of 60°. Using Snell's law with $n_{\text{air}} = 1.00$ and $n_{\text{water}} = 1.33$, calculate the refraction angle in water. Then, if the light must travel through 2.5 m of water to reach the bottom of a pool, determine the horizontal distance the light travels underwater.
Answer: _____ m
4.6
A fiber optic cable uses glass with $n = 1.48$ surrounded by a cladding with $n = 1.46$. Light travels through the glass at an angle of 30° from the normal at the glass-cladding interface. Calculate the refraction angle in the cladding using Snell's law. If refraction occurs (does not undergo total internal reflection), what is the critical angle for this interface?
Answer: Critical angle = _____ degrees
4.7
Light passes through three layers: air ($n_1 = 1.00$) → glass ($n_2 = 1.52$) → water ($n_3 = 1.33$). The incident angle in air is 45°. Using Snell's law sequentially, calculate: (a) the refraction angle in glass, and (b) the refraction angle in water when light exits the glass-water interface.
Answer: Glass refraction angle = _____ degrees
4.8
A prism made of glass ($n = 1.50$) has an apex angle of 60°. Light enters at an incident angle of 40°. Calculate the refraction angle as light enters the glass using Snell's law. Then, based on the prism geometry, determine if the refracted ray will hit the opposite face of the prism and at what angle to the normal.
Answer: Refraction angle = _____ degrees
Scenario 3: Interference, Standing Waves, and Resonance
4.9
Two speakers 3.0 m apart emit sound waves with frequency 170 Hz. Sound travels at 340 m/s in air. At a point 2.0 m from one speaker and 2.5 m from the other, calculate: (a) the wavelength of the sound, (b) the path difference, and (c) determine if constructive or destructive interference occurs at this point.
Answer: Wavelength = _____ m
4.10
A guitar string 0.64 m long vibrates at its fundamental frequency with a wavelength of 1.28 m. Calculate: (a) the frequency if the wave speed in the string is 320 m/s, (b) the wavelength at the second harmonic, and (c) the frequency of the second harmonic.
Answer: Fundamental frequency = _____ Hz
4.11
A standing wave forms on a string with length 2.0 m and wave speed 400 m/s. For the third harmonic (3 antinodes): (a) calculate the wavelength, (b) calculate the frequency, and (c) determine the number and spacing of nodes in this standing wave pattern.
Answer: Wavelength = _____ m
4.12
A resonance tube (open at one end, closed at the other) has a length of 0.34 m. Sound with frequency 500 Hz resonates in the tube. Calculate: (a) the wavelength using $v = f\lambda$ with sound speed 340 m/s, (b) verify this matches the resonance condition for a tube with one closed end, and (c) predict the next resonant frequency for this tube.
Answer: Wavelength = _____ m
