Scenario 1: Reflection & Law of Reflection
4.1
A laser beam is aimed at a plane mirror. The angle between the incident beam and the surface of the mirror is 65°. Calculate the angle of incidence (measured from the normal) and then determine the angle of reflection.
Angle of incidence: _____ degrees; Angle of reflection: _____ degrees
4.2
A periscope uses two mirrors arranged at 45° angles. If light enters the first mirror at an angle of 30° from the normal, trace the path through both mirrors. At what angle does the light exit the second mirror?
Light exits at: _____ degrees from the normal
4.3
A sound wave in air reflects off a canyon wall. The wave travels at 343 m/s and takes 8 seconds for the echo to return. Calculate the distance to the wall and determine the angle of reflection if the angle of incidence was 25°.
Distance: _____ m; Angle of reflection: _____ degrees
4.4
In a billiards game, a cue ball strikes a wall at an angle of incidence of 35° from the normal. After reflecting, the ball travels 2 meters before hitting another wall at an angle of incidence of 50°. Determine the angle at which it reflects from the second wall and explain the geometric relationship.
Angle from second wall: _____ degrees
4.5
Two plane mirrors are positioned at a 60° angle to each other. A light ray enters the first mirror at 20° from the normal. Calculate the angle of reflection from the first mirror, then determine the angle of incidence on the second mirror.
Reflection from mirror 1: _____ degrees; Incidence on mirror 2: _____ degrees
4.6
A 200-meter tall lighthouse beam reflects off the ocean surface at 12° from the normal. If an observer on a ship is positioned such that the reflected beam reaches them, calculate what angle the reflected beam makes with the water surface and determine the angle of incidence.
Angle with surface: _____ degrees; Angle of incidence: _____ degrees
Scenario 2: Refraction & Snell's Law
4.7
Light travels from diamond ($n_1 = 2.42$) into air ($n_2 = 1.00$) at an angle of 20° from the normal inside the diamond. Calculate the refraction angle in air and determine if total internal reflection occurs (critical angle for diamond is 24.4°).
Refraction angle: _____ degrees; Total internal reflection: Yes/No
4.8
A light ray enters water ($n = 1.33$) from air at 45° from the normal. Calculate the refraction angle in water. Then, if this refracted ray exits the water into oil ($n = 1.46$), determine the new refraction angle.
Refraction in water: _____ degrees; Refraction in oil: _____ degrees
4.9
An underwater explorer uses a light source to signal at an angle of 30° from the normal in water ($n = 1.33$). Calculate the refraction angle in air ($n = 1.00$) and determine the apparent depth of an object 15 meters deep when viewed from above at this angle.
Refraction angle: _____ degrees; Apparent depth: _____ m
4.10
Glass ($n = 1.52$) and water ($n = 1.33$) are in contact. Light travels from glass into water at an angle of incidence of 35° from the normal. Calculate the refraction angle, and determine the percentage change in the bending angle compared to light going from glass directly to air.
Refraction angle: _____ degrees; Percentage difference: _____ %
4.11
A fiber optic cable uses glass ($n = 1.50$) as the core surrounded by cladding ($n = 1.48$). Light enters the cable from air at 60° from the normal. Calculate the refraction angle in the glass core and the critical angle for total internal reflection at the glass-cladding interface.
Refraction angle: _____ degrees; Critical angle: _____ degrees
4.12
Light passes through three media in sequence: air ($n_1 = 1.00$) → glass ($n_2 = 1.50$) → diamond ($n_3 = 2.42$). The incident angle in air is 30°. Calculate the refraction angles in glass and diamond, showing how light bends more severely in each successive medium.
In glass: _____ degrees; In diamond: _____ degrees
Scenario 3: Constructive & Destructive Interference
4.13
Two speakers produce sound waves at 440 Hz (wavelength = 0.782 m). An observer stands at a point where the path difference is 1.564 m from speaker A and 3.128 m from speaker B. Determine the path difference and identify whether the interference is constructive or destructive.
Path difference: _____ m; Interference type: Constructive/Destructive
4.14
In a double-slit experiment with light ($\lambda = 600$ nm), interference fringes appear on a screen 2 meters away. The first bright fringe appears at a distance of 0.6 mm from the central maximum. If the slit separation is 0.5 mm, verify this distance and determine the order of the bright fringe.
Fringe order: $n$ = _____ ; Path difference: _____ nm
4.15
Two water waves originate from sources 3 meters apart and have wavelength 0.5 m. At a point on a line parallel to the sources, the distances are 5 m and 4 m respectively. Calculate the path difference and determine the interference pattern (constructive, destructive, or neither).
Path difference: _____ m; Interference: Constructive/Destructive/Neither
4.16
A tuning fork produces sound at 256 Hz with wavelength 1.34 m. Two identical tuning forks are placed such that constructive interference occurs at a certain point with path difference of 2.68 m. Determine the order and verify that this is constructive interference. How many wavelengths fit in this path difference?
Order: $n$ = _____; Number of wavelengths: _____
4.17
In a ripple tank, two vibrating sources create waves with wavelength 0.25 m. At point P, the waves travel distances of 2.5 m and 3.75 m respectively. At point Q, they travel 4.0 m and 4.5 m. Calculate path differences at both points and determine the interference type at each location.
Point P path difference: _____ m (___________); Point Q path difference: _____ m (___________)
4.18
Radar waves with frequency 10 GHz and speed $3 \times 10^8$ m/s are used in a radar system. Two reflected signals arrive with a path difference of 0.03 m. Calculate the wavelength, determine if this represents constructive or destructive interference, and find what order of interference this is.
Wavelength: _____ m; Order: $n$ = _____; Interference: Constructive/Destructive
Scenario 4: Combined Wave Phenomena
4.19
A light ray enters water at 40° from the normal ($n_{water} = 1.33$), travels through water, and then refracts again when exiting into air. Calculate the refraction angle in water and then the exit angle in air, showing how the ray returns to its original direction.
In water: _____ degrees; Exiting to air: _____ degrees
4.20
A mirror and a refracting surface are arranged perpendicular to each other. Light hits the mirror at 30° from the normal, reflects, and then hits the refracting surface (air to glass, $n = 1.50$) at the reflected angle. Calculate the reflection angle, incident angle on glass, and refraction angle in glass.
Reflection: _____ degrees; Incident on glass: _____ degrees; In glass: _____ degrees
4.21
Two coherent light sources (440 nm) are separated by 2 mm and shine on a screen 1 meter away. Calculate the wavelength in space, the path difference for the first bright fringe, and the fringe spacing on the screen.
Wavelength: _____ nm; Path difference: _____ nm; Fringe spacing: _____ mm
4.22
A laser beam reflects off a rough surface, creating diffuse reflection. If the surface has irregularities with dimensions of 0.5 mm and the laser wavelength is 633 nm, determine if diffuse reflection will occur and explain why. Calculate the ratio of surface roughness to wavelength.
Roughness/wavelength ratio: _____; Diffuse reflection: Yes/No
4.23
An optical system uses a glass prism ($n = 1.52$) to disperse white light. A ray enters at 45° from the normal. Calculate the refraction angle at the first surface, and then determine if total internal reflection occurs at the back surface (opposite side) if light hits at 30° from the normal inside the glass.
Refraction angle: _____ degrees; Critical angle: _____ degrees; TIR occurs: Yes/No
4.24
A submarine sonar system detects a fish using 40 kHz ultrasound waves. The wave refracts at the salt water-fresh water boundary ($n_1 = 1.025$, $n_2 = 1.000$). If the incident angle is 25° from the normal and wavelength in salt water is 0.037 m, calculate the refraction angle, the wavelength in fresh water, and the frequency of the wave.
Refraction angle: _____ degrees; Wavelength: _____ m; Frequency: _____ Hz
