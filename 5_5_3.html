16 questions | Numeric response format | Formulas for wave equation, speed of sound, frequency-period, and Doppler effect
Formula 1: Wave Equation
Formula: $v = f\lambda$

Variables: $v$ = wave speed (m/s), $f$ = frequency (Hz), $\lambda$ = wavelength (m)
3.1
A sound wave travels through air at a speed of 343 m/s. If the frequency is 512 Hz, what is the wavelength?
Answer: _____ m
3.2
The lowest note on a bass guitar has a wavelength of 10.7 m in air (speed = 343 m/s). Determine the frequency of this note.
Answer: _____ Hz
3.3
Determine the speed of sound in an unknown medium if a 1,000 Hz sound wave has a wavelength of 0.75 m in that medium.
Answer: _____ m/s
3.4
Ultrasound with a frequency of 3.0 MHz is used in medical imaging. The speed of ultrasound in tissue is 1,540 m/s. What is the wavelength in tissue?
Answer: _____ m (or _____ mm)
Formula 2: Speed of Sound in Air (Temperature Dependence)
Formula: $v = 331 + 0.6T$

Variables: $v$ = speed of sound (m/s), $T$ = temperature (°C)
3.5
Calculate the speed of sound in air on a hot summer day when the temperature is 35°C.
Answer: _____ m/s
3.6
On a cold winter morning, the temperature is −5°C. What is the speed of sound in air at this temperature?
Answer: _____ m/s
3.7
At what temperature would the speed of sound in air be 340 m/s?
Answer: _____ °C
3.8
A sound engineer needs sound to travel at 346 m/s for a recording project. Calculate the required temperature.
Answer: _____ °C
Formula 3: Frequency-Period Relationship
Formula: $f = \frac{1}{T}$ or $T = \frac{1}{f}$

Variables: $f$ = frequency (Hz), $T$ = period (seconds)
3.9
A tuning fork vibrates with a period of 0.0025 seconds. Determine the frequency of the tuning fork.
Answer: _____ Hz
3.10
The A note above middle C on a piano has a frequency of 440 Hz. What is the period of oscillation for this note?
Answer: _____ s
3.11
A dog whistle produces sound at a frequency of 25 kHz. Calculate the period of vibration.
Answer: _____ s (or _____ μs)
3.12
A speaker cone oscillates back and forth with a period of 0.002 s. Determine the frequency of the sound produced.
Answer: _____ Hz
Formula 4: Doppler Effect (Source Approaching Observer)
Formula: $f_{obs} = f_{source} \left(\frac{v_{sound}}{v_{sound} - v_{source}}\right)$

Variables: $f_{obs}$ = observed frequency (Hz), $f_{source}$ = source frequency (Hz), $v_{sound}$ = speed of sound (m/s), $v_{source}$ = source speed (m/s)
Note: Use minus (−) when source approaches; use plus (+) when source recedes
3.13
An ambulance siren emits a frequency of 800 Hz while moving toward a stationary observer at 20 m/s. The speed of sound is 340 m/s. What frequency does the observer hear?
Answer: _____ Hz
3.14
A fire truck with siren frequency 600 Hz moves toward you at 15 m/s. The speed of sound is 343 m/s. Calculate the frequency you hear.
Answer: _____ Hz
3.15
The ambulance from question 3.13 now moves away from the observer at 20 m/s. Using the same conditions (source frequency = 800 Hz, sound speed = 340 m/s), what frequency does the observer hear as it recedes?
Answer: _____ Hz
3.16
A train whistle has a frequency of 500 Hz. As the train moves away from a station observer at 25 m/s, what frequency does the observer measure? (speed of sound = 340 m/s)
Answer: _____ Hz
