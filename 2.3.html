Bank 3 – Single-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A car accelerates from 10 m/s to 25 m/s in 5.0 s. Calculate the average acceleration.
2. Determine the average acceleration of a bicycle whose velocity changes from 3.0 m/s to 12 m/s over a 6.0 s time interval.
3. Starting from rest, an object reaches a velocity of 20 m/s after accelerating for 4.0 s. Find the average acceleration.
4. A runner increases speed from 5.0 m/s to 8.0 m/s in 2.0 s. Calculate the average acceleration during this sprint.
5. During braking, a train's velocity decreases from 30 m/s to 15 m/s over a 10 s period. Determine the average acceleration.
6. Find the average acceleration of a car as it slows from 25 m/s to 10 m/s in 5.0 s.
7. An object traveling at 18 m/s comes to rest in 6.0 s. Calculate the average acceleration.
8. A ball rolling across the floor slows from 12 m/s to a complete stop in 3.0 s. Determine the acceleration.
9. Calculate the velocity after 5.0 s for an object that starts from rest and accelerates at 4.0 m/s².
10. A car initially traveling at 15 m/s accelerates at 3.0 m/s² for 4.0 s. Find the final velocity.
11. An object moving at 8.0 m/s undergoes acceleration of 2.5 m/s² for 6.0 s. Determine the final velocity.
12. Find the final velocity of a bicycle that accelerates at 1.5 m/s² for 8.0 s, given that its initial velocity is 10 m/s.
13. An object with velocity 20 m/s experiences acceleration of -4.0 m/s² for 3.0 s. Find the final velocity.
14. A delivery truck traveling at 25 m/s applies its brakes, producing an acceleration of -5.0 m/s². Calculate the velocity after 2.0 s of braking.
15. How much time is required for a car to accelerate from 10 m/s to 30 m/s if it accelerates at 5.0 m/s²?
16. An object slows from 24 m/s to 6.0 m/s while experiencing an acceleration of -3.0 m/s². Calculate the time interval.
17. Starting from rest, a train accelerates at 2.5 m/s² until it reaches 15 m/s. Determine the time required.
18. A car traveling at 40 m/s brakes with an acceleration of -8.0 m/s² until it stops. Find the time needed to stop.
19. During a 4.0 s interval, an object's velocity changes from 5.0 m/s to 17 m/s. Calculate the average acceleration.
20. A rocket lifts off from rest and reaches a velocity of 120 m/s after 15 s. Determine the average acceleration during liftoff.
21. Calculate the average acceleration experienced by a motorcycle whose velocity increases from 14 m/s to 32 m/s in 9.0 s.
22. An airplane on approach slows from 80 m/s to 20 m/s over a 12 s period. Find the average acceleration.
23. An object initially moving at 6.0 m/s accelerates at 3.5 m/s² for 10 s. Determine the final velocity.
24. Determine the final velocity of a skateboarder who accelerates at 0.80 m/s² for 5.0 s, starting with an initial velocity of 4.0 m/s.

Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. Starting from rest, a car accelerates at 3.0 m/s² for 8.0 s and then maintains constant velocity for the next 5.0 s. Calculate the final velocity.
2. A bicycle moving at 6.0 m/s accelerates at 2.0 m/s² for 4.0 s, then experiences deceleration of -1.5 m/s² for 6.0 s. Determine the final velocity.
3. A runner maintains a velocity of 5.0 m/s, then accelerates at 1.5 m/s² for 6.0 s. After maintaining this new velocity for 10 s, the runner decelerates at -2.0 m/s² for 3.0 s. Find the final velocity.
4. During the first phase of motion, a train traveling at 25 m/s decelerates at -3.0 m/s² for 5.0 s. The train then accelerates at 2.0 m/s² for 7.0 s. Calculate the final velocity.
5. An object starts from rest and accelerates at 4.0 m/s² for 3.0 s. Following this acceleration phase, the object decelerates at -2.0 m/s² for 4.0 s. Determine the final velocity.
6. A delivery driver accelerates a car from 15 m/s at a rate of 2.5 m/s² for 4.0 s, maintains constant velocity for 8.0 s, then brakes with acceleration -5.0 m/s² for 2.0 s. Find the final velocity.
7. Calculate the velocity after the constant velocity phase for a motorcycle that accelerates from rest at 5.0 m/s² for 6.0 s, then continues at constant velocity for 12 s.
8. An airplane cruising at 60 m/s accelerates at 4.0 m/s² for 10 s during takeoff climb. The plane then reduces power and decelerates at -2.0 m/s² for 15 s. Determine the final velocity.
9. A skater gliding at 8.0 m/s experiences friction causing deceleration of -1.0 m/s² for 5.0 s, then pushes off to accelerate at 3.0 m/s² for 4.0 s. Find the final velocity.
10. Starting from rest, a car accelerates at 2.0 m/s² for 10 s. The driver then brakes with acceleration -4.0 m/s² until the car stops. Calculate the time spent decelerating.
11. A ball is thrown upward with initial velocity 15 m/s. Throughout its flight, the ball experiences constant downward acceleration of -10 m/s². Calculate the velocity after 2.0 s.
12. A rocket launching from rest accelerates at 20 m/s² for the first 5.0 s, then continues accelerating at a reduced rate of 15 m/s² for 8.0 s. Determine the final velocity.
13. Find the final velocity of a cyclist who travels at 12 m/s, then slows down with acceleration -2.0 m/s² for 3.0 s, and finally speeds up with acceleration 1.5 m/s² for 8.0 s.
14. An object initially at 10 m/s accelerates at 3.0 m/s² for 4.0 s, then decelerates at -1.5 m/s² for 6.0 s, and finally maintains constant velocity for 5.0 s. Calculate the final velocity.
15. A commuter train moving at 30 m/s approaches a station and decelerates at -2.5 m/s² for 8.0 s. The train then maintains this reduced velocity for 6.0 s while entering the station. Find the velocity at the end.
16. A car accelerates from 20 m/s at 4.0 m/s² for 5.0 s. After reaching this new velocity, the car travels at constant velocity for 10 s. Calculate the displacement during the constant velocity portion.
17. An object accelerates from rest at 6.0 m/s² for 4.0 s. Calculate the average velocity during this acceleration period.
18. A sprinter starting from rest accelerates at 4.0 m/s² for 3.0 s, maintains constant velocity for 5.0 s, and then slows down with acceleration -2.5 m/s² for 4.0 s. Find the final velocity.
19. An object traveling at 18 m/s experiences acceleration -3.0 m/s² for 4.0 s. Following this, the object undergoes acceleration 2.0 m/s² for 6.0 s. Calculate the final velocity.
20. A driver traveling at 25 m/s notices a yellow light ahead and applies the brakes, producing acceleration -5.0 m/s². The driver brakes until reaching a velocity of 10 m/s. Calculate the time required for this deceleration.
21. Starting from rest, a bicycle accelerates at 1.5 m/s² for 8.0 s. The cyclist then coasts and slows down with acceleration -0.50 m/s² for 12 s. Determine the final velocity.
22. A runner moving at 7.0 m/s accelerates at 0.50 m/s² for 10 s during the final stretch of a race. The runner maintains this increased velocity for the remaining 15 s of the race. What is the velocity at the finish line?
