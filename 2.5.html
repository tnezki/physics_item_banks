Bank 3 – Single-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A position-time graph shows a straight line from (2.0 s, 4.0 m) to (8.0 s, 28 m). Calculate the velocity.
2. Find the velocity for an object whose position-time graph passes through points (0 s, 10 m) and (5.0 s, 35 m).
3. A physics teacher displays a position-time graph for analysis. Calculate the velocity when the graph shows a straight line from (1.0 s, 8.0 m) to (6.0 s, 33 m).
4. A velocity-time graph shows a straight line from (0 s, 5.0 m/s) to (10 s, 25 m/s). Calculate the acceleration.
5. Determine the acceleration from a velocity-time graph that passes through (2.0 s, 12 m/s) and (7.0 s, 32 m/s).
6. An engineer analyzing motion data sees a velocity-time graph with a straight line from (1.0 s, 6.0 m/s) to (5.0 s, 18 m/s). Find the acceleration.
7. A velocity-time graph shows constant velocity of 15 m/s from t = 0 to t = 8.0 s. Calculate the displacement.
8. Find the displacement when a velocity-time graph displays a horizontal line at v = 20 m/s for 6.0 s.
9. A car's data recorder shows constant velocity of 25 m/s for 12 s. Determine the displacement from the velocity-time graph.
10. A velocity-time graph forms a triangle with vertices at (0 s, 0 m/s), (10 s, 0 m/s), and (10 s, 30 m/s). Calculate the displacement.
11. Calculate the displacement for a triangular region on a velocity-time graph with base 8.0 s and height 24 m/s.
12. A sprint coach analyzes an athlete's motion. Find the displacement when the velocity-time graph shows a triangle with base 5.0 s and height 12 m/s.
13. A position-time graph shows a straight line from (0 s, 0 m) to (12 s, 48 m). Calculate the velocity.
14. Determine the velocity from a position-time graph passing through points (3.0 s, 15 m) and (9.0 s, 39 m).
15. A robotics team programs motion and records data. Find the velocity when their position-time graph goes from (4.0 s, 20 m) to (10 s, 50 m).
16. A velocity-time graph shows a straight line from (0 s, 0 m/s) to (8.0 s, 32 m/s). Calculate the acceleration.
17. Find the acceleration when a velocity-time graph increases linearly from 10 m/s at t = 0 to 30 m/s at t = 5.0 s.
18. A test driver records vehicle performance. Determine the acceleration from a velocity-time graph showing velocity changing from 8.0 m/s to 28 m/s in 4.0 s.
19. A velocity-time graph shows constant velocity at 18 m/s for 10 s. Calculate the displacement.
20. Find the displacement for a rectangular region on a velocity-time graph with width 15 s and height 12 m/s.
21. An assembly line engineer monitors a conveyor belt. Calculate the displacement when the velocity-time graph shows v = 8.0 m/s for 25 s.
22. A velocity-time graph displays a triangle from rest, reaching 40 m/s after 10 s. Calculate the displacement.
23. Determine the displacement for a triangular velocity-time graph with base 12 s and height 36 m/s.
24. A cyclist's velocity-time graph shows uniform acceleration from 0 to 20 m/s over 8.0 s. Find the displacement during this acceleration.

Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A velocity-time graph shows three segments: velocity increases from 0 to 15 m/s in 3.0 s (triangle), remains at 15 m/s for 6.0 s (rectangle), then decreases to 0 in 3.0 s (triangle). Calculate the total displacement.
2. Find the total displacement for a velocity-time graph with two regions: a triangle from 0 to 20 m/s over 4.0 s, followed by a rectangle at 20 m/s for 8.0 s.
3. A delivery truck's motion shows three phases on a velocity-time graph. Calculate the total displacement for: acceleration from 0 to 12 m/s in 4.0 s, constant velocity at 12 m/s for 10 s, then deceleration to rest in 3.0 s.
4. A velocity-time graph shows a line from (0 s, 10 m/s) to (6.0 s, 34 m/s). Calculate the acceleration, then find the displacement during this time.
5. Determine both the acceleration and displacement from a velocity-time graph showing a straight line from (2.0 s, 8.0 m/s) to (8.0 s, 32 m/s).
6. An aerospace engineer analyzes test flight data. Find the acceleration and displacement for a velocity-time graph segment going from 15 m/s at t = 0 to 45 m/s at t = 10 s.
7. A position-time graph passes through (0 s, 5.0 m) and (10 s, 55 m). Calculate the velocity, then determine how far the object travels in the next 8.0 s at this constant velocity.
8. Find the velocity from a position-time graph through points (3.0 s, 12 m) and (9.0 s, 48 m). Using this velocity, calculate the additional displacement if motion continues for 15 s.
9. A manufacturing robot's position-time graph shows movement from (2.0 s, 10 m) to (8.0 s, 40 m). Calculate the velocity, then find the time needed to travel an additional 75 m at this velocity.
10. A velocity-time graph forms a trapezoid: velocity starts at 10 m/s, increases linearly to 20 m/s over 4.0 s, remains at 20 m/s for 6.0 s, then drops to 0 instantly. Calculate the total displacement.
11. Calculate the total displacement for a trapezoid-shaped velocity-time graph: starts at 8.0 m/s, increases to 18 m/s over 3.0 s, stays at 18 m/s for 5.0 s, then decreases to 8.0 m/s over 2.0 s.
12. A race car driver's telemetry shows a velocity-time trapezoid. Find the displacement when velocity goes from 15 m/s to 30 m/s in 5.0 s, maintains 30 m/s for 10 s, then drops to 15 m/s in 5.0 s.
13. A velocity-time graph shows two triangular regions: one above the time axis from 0 to 15 m/s over 6.0 s, then one below from 0 to -10 m/s over 4.0 s. Calculate the total displacement.
14. Find the net displacement when a velocity-time graph shows: acceleration from 0 to 20 m/s over 5.0 s, then deceleration through zero to -12 m/s over 8.0 s.
15. An oscillating mechanism's velocity changes from +18 m/s to -18 m/s linearly over 9.0 s. Calculate the displacement during this motion.
16. A velocity-time graph shows a line from (0 s, 6.0 m/s) to (10 s, 26 m/s). Calculate the acceleration, then find the average velocity during this interval.
17. Determine the acceleration from a velocity-time graph going from 12 m/s to 32 m/s in 5.0 s, then calculate how long it takes to reach 52 m/s at this acceleration.
18. A subway train's computer logs velocity data. Find both acceleration and displacement when velocity increases from 10 m/s to 25 m/s over 6.0 s with constant acceleration.
19. A position-time graph shows points (0 s, 8.0 m) and (12 s, 68 m). Calculate the velocity, then find the position at t = 20 s if motion continues uniformly.
20. Find the velocity from a position-time graph through (4.0 s, 20 m) and (10 s, 56 m), then calculate the displacement from t = 10 s to t = 18 s.
21. A conveyor system's position-time graph passes through (5.0 s, 30 m) and (15 s, 90 m). Calculate the velocity, then determine how much time is needed to move an additional 120 m.
22. A velocity-time graph shows acceleration from 0 to 24 m/s over 8.0 s, followed by 10 s at constant velocity, then deceleration to 12 m/s over 4.0 s. Calculate the displacement during each phase, then find the total displacement.
