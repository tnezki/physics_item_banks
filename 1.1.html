Bank 3 – Single-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

2. A sample occupies 25 cm³ and has a mass of 200 g. Determine its density.
3. Determine the mass of a liquid that has a density of 2.0 g/cm³ and fills a volume of 150 cm³.
4. A solid has a density of 7.5 g/cm³. If its volume is 8.0 cm³, find the mass of the solid.
5. Calculate the volume of a material with a mass of 90 g and a density of 3.0 g/cm³.
6. A block of metal has a mass of 540 g and a volume of 60 cm³. Determine the density of the block.
7. Find the temperature in degrees Fahrenheit when the temperature is 25 °C.
8. A weather station records a temperature of 10 °C. Convert this temperature to degrees Fahrenheit.
9. Determine the temperature in degrees Celsius for a measurement of 68 °F.
10. A laboratory thermometer reads 95 °F. Convert this value to degrees Celsius.
11. Calculate the density of a fluid with a mass of 300 g and a volume of 120 cm³.
12. A substance has a density of 4.0 g/cm³ and a volume of 50 cm³. Determine the mass of the substance.
13. Find the volume of a sample that has a mass of 180 g and a density of 6.0 g/cm³.
14. A metal sample has a mass of 96 g and occupies 12 cm³. Determine its density.
15. Convert the temperature to Fahrenheit for a reading of −5 °C.
16. A temperature reading is 32 °F. Determine the equivalent temperature in degrees Celsius.
17. Calculate the mass of a material that has a density of 1.5 g/cm³ and a volume of 200 cm³.
18. A sample occupies 10 cm³ and has a mass of 85 g. Determine the density of the sample.
19. Determine the temperature in degrees Celsius when the temperature is 212 °F.
20. A material has a density of 0.80 g/cm³ and a mass of 160 g. Calculate the volume of the material.

Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. Determine the density of a substance that completely fills a rectangular container measuring 10 cm by 5 cm by 4 cm and has a mass of 400 g.
2. A liquid with a mass of 300 g fills a cylindrical container with a radius of 5 cm and a height of 6 cm. Calculate the density of the liquid.
3. Susie rides her bike at a constant speed. It took her 30 minutes to travel 8 miles to school. How long will it take her to travel 11 miles to her friend’s house at the same speed?
4. Calculate the travel time for a runner who moves at a constant speed, given that the runner covered 6 miles in 24 minutes and now needs to travel 10 miles.
5. A rectangular solid measures 8 cm by 5 cm by 5 cm and has a mass of 500 g. Determine the density of the solid.
6. What is the change in temperature in degrees Fahrenheit if a liquid heats at a constant rate of 2 °C per hour for 7 hours?
7. A substance with a mass of 240 g fills a box that is 6 cm long, 5 cm wide, and 4 cm tall. Calculate the density of the substance.
8. A car travels 120 miles in 2 hours at a constant speed. How long will it take the car to travel 90 miles at the same speed?
9. Determine the density of a liquid that has a mass of 180 g and fills a container whose volume is found by multiplying its dimensions: 6 cm × 5 cm × 5 cm.
10. A liquid cools at a constant rate of 3 °C per hour. After 4 hours, what is the total temperature change expressed in degrees Fahrenheit?
11. Calculate the density of a material that fills a box measuring 12 cm by 5 cm by 3 cm and has a mass of 360 g.
12. A cyclist rides 15 km in 45 minutes at a steady speed. How long will it take the cyclist to ride 22 km at the same speed?
13. A solid object has a mass of 270 g and occupies a container with a volume of 90 cm³. Determine the density of the object.
14. Determine the change in temperature in degrees Fahrenheit if a liquid warms at a rate of 1.5 °C per hour for 10 hours.
15. A box measuring 8 cm by 8 cm by 5 cm is filled with a material that has a mass of 640 g. Calculate the density of the material.
16. A bus travels 18 miles in 36 minutes at a constant speed. How long will it take the bus to travel 25 miles at the same speed?
17. Calculate the density of a sample that has a mass of 450 g and occupies a container with a volume of 150 cm³.
18. A liquid is heated steadily at 4 °C per hour. After 5 hours, what is the total temperature change in degrees Fahrenheit?
19. A rectangular container measures 10 cm by 4 cm by 3 cm and holds a substance with a mass of 360 g. Determine the density of the substance.
20. How long will it take a runner to travel 9 miles if the runner travels 5 miles in 20 minutes at a constant speed?
