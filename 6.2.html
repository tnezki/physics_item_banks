14. Light travels from crown glass ($n = 1.52$) into flint glass ($n = 1.65$) at 28.0° to the normal. Calculate the angle of refraction in flint glass in degrees.
15. A light beam enters water ($n = 1.33$) from air at 48.0°. After traveling through 1.5 m of water, it enters a glass block ($n = 1.50$) below. If the water-glass boundary is parallel to the air-water boundary, calculate the angle of refraction in the glass in degrees.
16. Calculate the new wavelength in a material with $n = 2.00$ when light with a wavelength of 500 nm travels through air ($n = 1.00$) and enters this material.
17. A laser beam passes through a stack of three transparent materials: first with $n = 1.30$, second with $n = 1.50$, and third with $n = 1.70$. The incident angle in the first material is 25.0°. Calculate the angle of refraction in the third material in degrees.
18. Determine the angle between the refracted ray and the surface itself when light enters a material with $n = 1.45$ from air at 42.0° and refracts.
19. A jeweler tests how light behaves in a diamond. A beam of light in air ($n = 1.00$) strikes a diamond ($n = 2.42$) at 30.0° to the normal. Calculate the angle of refraction in diamond, then determine the speed of light in the diamond in m/s. Give the speed as your answer.
20. Light passes from air into an unknown liquid at 50.0° and refracts to 32.0°. Calculate the index of refraction of the liquid, then calculate the speed of light in this liquid in m/s. Give the speed as your answer.
21. Calculate the total change in angle from initial direction in air to final direction in air when a light ray enters a triangular glass prism ($n = 1.50$) at 40.0° to the normal on one face, refracts through the glass, strikes the opposite face at 25.0° to that surface's normal, and exits into air.
