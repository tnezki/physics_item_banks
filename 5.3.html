Bank 3 – Single-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A wave reflects off a surface at 30°. According to the law of reflection, what is the angle of incidence?
2. Determine the angle of reflection when light strikes a mirror at an incident angle of 45°.
3. A physics student observes a standing wave pattern on a 2.0 m string with 4 nodes (including the ends). Calculate the wavelength. (Distance between nodes is $\lambda/2$)
4. A string 3.0 m long vibrates with a standing wave pattern showing 3 antinodes. Calculate the wavelength.
5. Two waves interfere constructively at a point in space. One wave has amplitude 4.0 cm and the other has amplitude 3.0 cm. What is the maximum resulting amplitude?
6. Calculate the resulting amplitude when two waves with equal amplitudes of 5.0 cm meet 180° out of phase.
7. In medium A, a wave travels at 20 m/s with wavelength 4.0 m. Upon entering medium B, the wavelength becomes 2.0 m. Calculate the wave speed in medium B using the fact that frequency remains constant.
8. Determine the wavelength of a standing wave that has adjacent nodes separated by 0.50 m.
9. Two sound waves with amplitudes of 2.0 units and 1.5 units meet and interfere constructively. Calculate the maximum amplitude.
10. A wave traveling through air has wavelength 3.0 m at speed 340 m/s. Calculate the new wavelength when this wave enters water where it travels at 1500 m/s.
11. A musician studies standing waves on a 1.5 m guitar string fixed at both ends. The pattern shows 6 nodes total (including the ends). Determine the wavelength.
12. Light reflects from a mirror surface. If the incident ray makes an angle of 25° with the normal, what is the angle of reflection from the normal?
13. Two wave pulses approach each other on a rope. One has amplitude +6.0 cm and the other has amplitude -4.0 cm. What is the displacement when they completely overlap?
14. Calculate the wavelength when a 2.4 m string vibrates in its fundamental mode with one antinode in the middle.
15. A standing wave pattern on a 6.0 m string has nodes at 0 m, 1.0 m, 2.0 m, 3.0 m, 4.0 m, 5.0 m, and 6.0 m. Calculate the number of wavelengths that fit on this string.
16. At a certain location in a room, two speakers emitting sound in phase with amplitude 8.0 units each produce constructive interference. What is the resulting amplitude?
17. A 500 Hz sound wave travels through air at 340 m/s, then enters steel where the speed becomes 5000 m/s. Calculate the wavelength in steel.
18. Determine the wavelength of a standing wave that has nodes located at 0 cm, 15 cm, 30 cm, and 45 cm along a string.
19. Light with wavelength 450 nm travels through water at 2.25 × 10⁸ m/s. Calculate the wavelength when it exits into air where light travels at 3.0 × 10⁸ m/s.
20. When two waves with amplitudes 3.5 cm and 2.5 cm meet 180° out of phase, what is the resulting amplitude?

Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A string 3.0 m long is fixed at both ends and vibrates in its second harmonic with 2 antinodes. Calculate the wavelength, then determine how many complete wavelengths fit on the string.
2. A physics lab studies wave propagation in different media. A 440 Hz sound wave travels at 340 m/s in air. Calculate its wavelength in air. Then determine what the wavelength becomes when the wave enters water traveling at 1500 m/s (frequency unchanged).
3. Two speakers placed 4.0 m apart emit sound waves in phase with wavelength 2.0 m. A listener stands at a point equidistant from both speakers. Calculate the path difference (should be zero). Then determine if this location experiences constructive or destructive interference.
4. Calculate the wave speed using $v = f\lambda$ for a standing wave on a 2.0 m string that has frequency 60 Hz and wavelength 0.80 m. Then determine how many nodes exist on this string (including the ends).
5. An optics experiment uses light with wavelength 600 nm in vacuum where $v = 3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. The light enters glass and travels at $2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the frequency in vacuum (which remains constant), then find the wavelength in glass.
6. Two coherent wave sources emit waves with amplitude 4.0 cm and wavelength 2.0 m. Determine if interference is constructive or destructive at a point where the path difference is 1.0 m. Then calculate the resulting amplitude.
7. A musician investigates standing waves on a 1.5 m string vibrating at 120 Hz. The pattern shows 3 antinodes. Calculate the wavelength, then determine the wave speed on the string.
8. Two loudspeakers separated by 8.0 m emit 340 Hz sound in phase. Sound speed is 340 m/s. Calculate the wavelength first. At a point 6.0 m from one speaker and 10 m from the other, calculate the path difference and determine the type of interference.
9. A rope 4.0 m long with both ends fixed forms a standing wave pattern with 5 nodes (including both ends). Calculate the wavelength. Then if the wave speed is 20 m/s, determine the frequency.
10. Light undergoes refraction when entering glass. In air the light has wavelength 480 nm and travels at $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the frequency (which stays constant). Then find the new wavelength in glass where light travels at $2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s.
11. An acoustics engineer studies wave propagation through three different media. In medium A, a wave travels at 10 m/s with wavelength 2.0 m. Calculate the frequency. Then determine the wavelength in medium B where speed is 15 m/s, and in medium C where speed is 20 m/s.
12. Two waves with equal amplitude $A = 5.0$ cm travel through a region. At point X they arrive in phase (constructive), at point Y they arrive 180° out of phase (destructive). Calculate the amplitude at point X and at point Y.
13. A standing wave pattern on a 6.0 m string shows nodes at 0 m, 2.0 m, 4.0 m, and 6.0 m. Calculate the wavelength. The frequency is 30 Hz. Determine the wave speed.
14. An acoustics lab studies standing waves in a tube. Antinodes appear at 0.25 m and 0.75 m from one end. Sound travels at 343 m/s through the air in the tube. Calculate the wavelength from the antinode spacing. Then determine the frequency.
15. Two microwave sources operate at frequency $1.0 \times 10^{10}$ Hz with speed $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the wavelength. At a location where path difference is 0.045 m, determine if interference is constructive or destructive (path difference must be a multiple of $\lambda$ for constructive).
16. A guitar string 2.5 m long vibrates in its 3rd harmonic with 3 antinodes. Calculate the wavelength for this mode. If the wave speed is 100 m/s, determine the frequency.
17. Water waves approach a barrier containing two small openings separated by 3.0 m. The waves have wavelength 1.5 m and frequency 2.0 Hz. Calculate the wave speed. At a point equidistant from both openings, determine the type of interference.
18. A laser beam with wavelength 630 nm in vacuum enters a medium where light speed decreases to 75% of its vacuum value. Calculate the new wavelength in the medium. Then determine the frequency using $c = 3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s in vacuum.
19. An engineering student investigates standing waves on a 1.8 m string with 4 antinodes. Calculate the wavelength. The string tension produces a wave speed of 72 m/s. Determine the frequency of vibration.
20. Radio antennas for a communication system emit waves in phase at 100 MHz ($1.0 \times 10^8$ Hz). Radio waves travel at $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the wavelength. At a receiving point where one antenna is 12 m away and the other is 15 m away, determine if interference is constructive or destructive.
