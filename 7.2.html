Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. Two point charges of $+4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and $-4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are separated by 0.50 m. Calculate the electric field strength at the midpoint between them in N/C.
2. Calculate the electric field at a point 0.30 m to the right of a $+6.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge and 0.40 m to the left of a $-3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge. The charges are 0.70 m apart on a horizontal line. Determine the net field strength in N/C.
3. A researcher designing particle detection equipment positions test charges. A charge of $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C is placed at the origin. Calculate the electric potential energy when a $+2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge is brought from infinity to a point 0.25 m away using $PE = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r}$ in joules.
4. The electric field between two parallel plates is $5.0 \times 10^4$ N/C and the plates are 0.030 m apart. Calculate the voltage between the plates in volts.
5. Two parallel plates are separated by 0.050 m and have a voltage of 500 V across them. Calculate the electric field strength between the plates in N/C.
6. An electron (charge $-1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C) is in a uniform electric field of $2.0 \times 10^4$ N/C. Calculate the force on the electron, then determine its acceleration using $F = ma$ where the electron mass is $9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg in m/s².
7. Determine the electric field strength at a point located 0.40 m from a $+8.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge and 0.30 m from a $-6.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge when the charges are positioned so the point is equidistant and the angle between the two field vectors is 90°. Calculate the magnitude in N/C.
8. A technician calibrating high-voltage equipment applies test conditions. A proton (charge $+1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C, mass $1.67 \times 10^{-27}$ kg) is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of 1000 V. Using energy conservation ($qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$), calculate the final speed in m/s.
9. Three charges are arranged at the corners of a right triangle: $+3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at the origin, $+4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at $(0.30, 0)$ m, and $-5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at $(0, 0.40)$ m. Calculate the magnitude of the net electric field at the origin in N/C.
10. Two charges of $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C each are located at $(-0.20, 0)$ m and $(+0.20, 0)$ m. Calculate the electric field strength at point $(0, 0.15)$ m in N/C.
11. Calculate how much work must be done to move a $+3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge from a point 0.50 m away from a $+6.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge to a point 0.25 m away from it. Use $W = q \Delta V$ where $\Delta V = k\frac{Q}{r_f} - k\frac{Q}{r_i}$ in joules.
12. A capacitor engineer tests plate configurations. The electric field between parallel plates is $8.0 \times 10^4$ N/C. An electron starting from rest at the negative plate reaches the positive plate 0.020 m away. First calculate the voltage, then find the electron's kinetic energy in joules.
13. Two parallel plates separated by 0.080 m have a voltage of 240 V. Calculate the field strength, then determine the force on a charge of $+4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C placed between them in newtons.
14. Calculate the distance from a $+9.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C point charge where the electric field strength is $3.0 \times 10^5$ N/C in meters.
15. A charge of $+2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C is moved from point A to point B, requiring 0.80 J of work. Calculate the voltage difference between points A and B in volts.
16. Determine the magnitude of charge on each plate when parallel plates separated by 0.040 m have voltage 600 V and create a uniform field. First calculate the field, then use $\sigma = \epsilon_0 E$ where $\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}$ C²/(N·m²) to find surface charge density in C/m². (Note: Just find field strength in N/C for this problem.)
17. A physics researcher analyzes field configurations. Three charges lie on a line: $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at $x = 0$ m, $-8.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at $x = 0.40$ m, and $+3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C at $x = 0.80$ m. Calculate the electric field at $x = 0.40$ m in N/C.
18. An alpha particle (charge $+3.20 \times 10^{-19}$ C, mass $6.64 \times 10^{-27}$ kg) accelerates through a potential difference of 2500 V. Calculate the kinetic energy gained in joules.
19. Calculate the speed an electron reaches after accelerating from rest through 500 V. Use $qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ where $q = -1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C and $m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg in m/s.
20. Two charges create a zero electric field at point P. A $+12 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge is 0.60 m from P, and a $-3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge is on the opposite side of P. Calculate the distance from P to the negative charge in meters.
21. Determine the work done moving a $+5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge from 0.80 m to 0.40 m from a $+10 \times 10^{-6}$ C point charge in joules.
22. An engineer optimizing voltage distribution systems tests field uniformity. A uniform field of $6.0 \times 10^4$ N/C exists between plates 0.12 m apart. Calculate the voltage, then determine how much kinetic energy a $+2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C charge gains when released from rest at the positive plate and allowed to reach the negative plate in joules.
