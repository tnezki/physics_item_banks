Scenario Group 1: Sonar Depth Measurement and Wave Properties
Context: A ship's sonar system emits sound pulses to detect objects and measure ocean depth. The speed of sound in seawater is 1,480 m/s. Use this information for all questions in this group unless otherwise specified.
4.1
A sonar pulse is sent from a ship and returns as an echo 4.0 seconds later. Calculate the depth of the ocean. (Hint: Sound travels down to the ocean floor and back up.)
Answer: _____ m
4.2
The sonar system operates at a frequency of 40 kHz to provide good resolution. Calculate the wavelength of the sonar signal in seawater.
Answer: _____ m (or _____ cm)
4.3
A submarine moving toward a sonar source at 10 m/s receives sonar signals at 40 kHz. The speed of sound in seawater is 1,480 m/s. What frequency does the submarine's receiver measure due to the Doppler effect? (Hint: The submarine is moving toward the source.)
Answer: _____ kHz
4.4
A sonar operator measures an echo return time of 2.5 seconds and needs to know both the ocean depth and the wavelength of the 50 kHz signal being used. Calculate both values. (Speed of sound in seawater = 1,480 m/s)
Answer: Depth = _____ m; Wavelength = _____ cm
