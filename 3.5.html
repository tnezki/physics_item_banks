Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A 50 kg object is placed on a 30° frictionless incline. Calculate the parallel component of weight pulling it down, then use Newton's Second Law to find the resulting acceleration. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 30° = 0.50$)
2. A 75 kg object sits on a 25° incline where friction is present ($\mu_k = 0.20$). First find the perpendicular component (normal force). Then calculate the friction force. Next, find the parallel component of weight. Finally, determine the net force and acceleration. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 25° = 0.42$, $\cos 25° = 0.91$)
3. A storage facility has a 100 kg crate on a 35° ramp. Calculate the parallel component pulling it down and the perpendicular component creating the normal force. Using $\mu_s = 0.50$, determine whether the crate is in danger of sliding. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 35° = 0.574$, $\cos 35° = 0.819$)
4. A 60 kg block slides down a steep 40° incline with kinetic friction where $\mu_k = 0.15$. Calculate in sequence: the normal force, the friction force, the parallel component of weight, the net force down the slope, and finally the acceleration. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 40° = 0.643$, $\cos 40° = 0.766$)
5. A skier with mass 55 kg descends a 20° slope where $\mu_k = 0.08$. Determine the perpendicular component (normal force). Then find the friction force opposing the motion. Calculate the parallel component down the slope. Next, find the net force and determine the acceleration. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 20° = 0.342$, $\cos 20° = 0.940$)
6. Two identical 40 kg blocks are placed on different inclines for comparison. Block A is on a 20° frictionless slope, Block B is on a 30° frictionless slope. Calculate the acceleration down the slope for each block and determine which accelerates faster. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 20° = 0.342$, $\sin 30° = 0.50$)
7. A 70 kg object is loaded onto a 30° incline for testing. Calculate the maximum static friction force that can prevent sliding (assume $\mu_s = 0.60$). Then determine whether the object will slide down the ramp. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 30° = 0.50$, $\cos 30° = 0.866$)
8. Engineers are designing a pulley system using a 120 kg box on a 25° incline. A rope pulls the box upward along the incline with 700 N of tension. Calculate the parallel component of weight opposing the motion. Then find the net force and determine the acceleration of the box. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 25° = 0.42$)
9. A 85 kg child plays on a slide inclined at 35° with coefficient of kinetic friction $\mu_k = 0.25$. Calculate the normal force first. Then find the friction force. Determine the parallel component of weight down the slide. Calculate the net force and the acceleration. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 35° = 0.574$, $\cos 35° = 0.819$)
10. A truck with a 2000 kg load is parked on a 12° hill. Calculate the parallel component of the load trying to roll the truck. With maximum static friction of 5000 N available, determine whether the truck will roll. Why or why not? (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 12° = 0.208$)
11. A 45 kg box is tested on a 40° incline with $\mu_s = 0.70$ and $\mu_k = 0.50$. Calculate the perpendicular component first. Then determine the maximum static friction. Find the parallel component of weight. Will the box slide? If it does, calculate the acceleration. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 40° = 0.643$, $\cos 40° = 0.766$)
12. A 90 kg skier attempts to maintain constant velocity (zero acceleration) on an 18° slope with kinetic friction $\mu_k = 0.10$. Calculate the friction force that would be available. Determine if this friction is sufficient to keep the skier at constant velocity. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 18° = 0.309$, $\cos 18° = 0.951$)
13. A loading dock operator needs to push a 150 kg appliance up a 15° ramp with an applied force of 800 N parallel to the ramp. Friction opposes with $\mu_k = 0.20$. Calculate the parallel component of weight opposing the push. Then find the friction force. Determine the net force and resulting acceleration. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 15° = 0.259$, $\cos 15° = 0.966$)
14. A 65 kg hiker with a 15 kg backpack climbs a 22° mountain trail at constant velocity. The friction force must equal the parallel component of the total weight. Calculate the total parallel component required. Then determine what coefficient of friction is needed. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 22° = 0.375$, $\cos 22° = 0.927$)
15. Two objects of different masses are connected on a frictionless 35° incline. A 30 kg block and a 50 kg block are linked by a rope. Calculate the acceleration of the system. Then determine the tension in the rope connecting them. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\sin 35° = 0.574$)
