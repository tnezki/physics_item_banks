Bank 3 – Single-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. Medical ultrasound operates at 3.0 MHz ($3.0 \times 10^6$ Hz) in tissue where sound travels at 1540 m/s. Calculate the wavelength.
2. A bat emits ultrasound at 50 kHz (50,000 Hz) in air where sound travels at 343 m/s. Determine the wavelength.
3. A sonar system emits a pulse that returns after 0.50 s. Sound travels at 1500 m/s in water. Calculate the total distance traveled by the pulse.
4. Calculate the distance to an object detected by sonar if the echo returns in 0.50 s (one-way distance). Sound speed in water is 1500 m/s.
5. An ultrasonic cleaner operates at 40 kHz (40,000 Hz) in water where sound travels at 1500 m/s. Find the wavelength.
6. A Doppler radar measures a frequency shift of 500 Hz from a moving vehicle. The transmitted frequency was 10,000 Hz. Calculate the observed frequency (transmitted + shift).
7. A concert hall has a reverberation time of 2.0 s. Sound travels 340 m/s. Calculate the distance sound travels during one reverberation time.
8. A guitar string vibrates at its fundamental frequency of 220 Hz. Calculate the frequency of the second harmonic (2 times fundamental).
9. Calculate the frequency of the third harmonic for a string with fundamental frequency 100 Hz.
10. An organ pipe produces a fundamental frequency of 128 Hz. Determine the fourth harmonic frequency.
11. Ultrasound imaging detects an echo from tissue 6.0 cm deep after time $t$. Sound travels at 1540 m/s in tissue. Calculate the time (convert cm to m first).
12. A dolphin's echolocation uses 120 kHz ($1.2 \times 10^5$ Hz) in water at 1500 m/s. Calculate the wavelength.
13. Calculate the distance to a canyon wall if your echo returns in 4.0 s. Sound travels at 340 m/s in air. (Remember: distance = half the total travel distance)
14. An acoustic engineer measures a sound level difference of 20 dB. This represents an intensity ratio of what factor? (Each 10 dB = 10× intensity)
15. A tuning fork produces 512 Hz. Calculate the period of one vibration using $T = \frac{1}{f}$.
16. Calculate the wavelength of a 20 Hz sound (very low bass, near lower limit of hearing) in air at 340 m/s.
17. A seismic P-wave travels at 6000 m/s with frequency 2.0 Hz. Determine its wavelength.
18. Ultrasound reflects off a moving blood cell, producing a Doppler shift of 1000 Hz. The original frequency was 2.0 MHz ($2.0 \times 10^6$ Hz). Calculate the observed frequency.
19. Calculate the wavelength of 20 kHz ultrasound (upper limit of human hearing) in air at 340 m/s.
20. A sound wave travels through steel at 5000 m/s with frequency 250 Hz. Find its wavelength.

Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. An ultrasound technician uses 5.0 MHz ($5.0 \times 10^6$ Hz) ultrasound in tissue (sound speed 1540 m/s). Calculate the wavelength. Then determine how many wavelengths fit in 10 cm of tissue depth. (Convert cm to m)
2. A bat detects an insect using 40 kHz ultrasound. The echo returns after 0.020 s. Sound travels at 343 m/s in air. Calculate the total distance sound traveled, then determine the distance to the insect (one-way).
3. A submarine uses sonar at 50 kHz (50,000 Hz) in water where sound travels at 1500 m/s. Calculate the wavelength of the sonar pulse. Then determine how long it takes to detect an object 3000 m away (round trip time).
4. A guitar string's fundamental frequency is 330 Hz. Calculate the wavelength if the string is 0.65 m long (wavelength = 2 × string length for fundamental). Then determine the wave speed on the string using $v = f\lambda$.
5. A concert hall is designed so sound takes 1.5 s to reach the back row. Sound travels at 343 m/s. Calculate the distance to the back row. Then determine what frequency sound would have a wavelength of 1.5 m in this hall.
6. Medical ultrasound at 3.5 MHz operates in tissue at 1540 m/s. Calculate the wavelength. An echo returns from 8.0 cm depth. Determine the round-trip time for the ultrasound pulse. (Convert cm to m)
7. A police radar operates at 10.5 GHz ($10.5 \times 10^9$ Hz). Electromagnetic waves travel at $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the wavelength. Then determine the Doppler frequency shift for a car approaching at 30 m/s using $\Delta f = \frac{2v_s f}{c}$.
8. An organ pipe closed at one end has length 0.50 m and produces its fundamental frequency. The wavelength is 4 times the pipe length for this configuration. Calculate the wavelength, then determine the frequency if sound travels at 343 m/s.
9. A seismologist detects P-waves (6000 m/s) and S-waves (3500 m/s) from an earthquake. The P-wave arrives 20 s before the S-wave. Let $d$ = distance. Set up equations: $t_P = d/6000$ and $t_S = d/3500$, with $t_S - t_P = 20$. Solve for distance $d$.
10. A dolphin's echolocation uses 100 kHz ($1.0 \times 10^5$ Hz) in water (1500 m/s). Calculate the wavelength. The dolphin detects a fish 150 m away. Determine the round-trip echo time.
11. Two tuning forks produce 440 Hz and 438 Hz. Calculate the beat frequency when sounded together. The sound level alternates from loud to quiet. Determine how many times per minute the sound reaches maximum loudness.
12. Noise-cancelling headphones detect engine noise at 200 Hz with amplitude 5.0 units. Calculate what frequency the headphones must generate. Then determine what amplitude (assuming perfect cancellation) and phase shift are required.
13. A sonar pulse at 20 kHz is emitted from a moving submarine traveling at 10 m/s toward a stationary target. Sound speed in water is 1500 m/s. Calculate the frequency detected by the target using $f' = f\frac{v}{v - v_s}$. Then determine the wavelength of the detected sound.
14. A recording studio treats one wall with acoustic foam that absorbs 80% of incident sound energy. A sound wave with intensity 100 W/m² hits the wall. Calculate the reflected intensity (20%). Then determine the transmitted intensity if none is transmitted through the wall.
15. An ultrasonic cleaner uses 40 kHz in water at 1480 m/s. Calculate the wavelength. The tank is 30 cm long. Determine how many complete wavelengths fit across the tank length. (Convert cm to m)
16. A seismic wave travels 4000 km through Earth. P-waves arrive at a seismometer 10 minutes before S-waves. P-wave speed is 8 km/s, S-wave speed is 4.5 km/s. Calculate the travel time for each wave type, then verify the difference is 10 minutes. (Convert minutes to seconds: 10 min = 600 s)
17. A physics student designs a noise barrier for a highway. Traffic noise is primarily 500 Hz. Sound travels at 340 m/s. Calculate the wavelength. For destructive interference, determine what barrier spacing would create a path difference of half a wavelength.
18. A bell rings at 400 Hz on a train moving at 25 m/s toward a station. Sound travels at 340 m/s. Calculate the frequency heard by a person at the station using $f' = f\frac{v}{v - v_s}$. Then calculate what they hear after the train passes using $f' = f\frac{v}{v + v_s}$.
19. An acoustic engineer designs a resonance tube for demonstrations. An open tube has fundamental wavelength equal to twice the tube length. For a tube 0.85 m long, calculate the fundamental wavelength, then determine the fundamental frequency if sound travels at 340 m/s.
20. A medical researcher uses 7.5 MHz ultrasound in tissue at 1540 m/s. Calculate the wavelength. To image a structure 12 cm deep requires the pulse to travel there and back. Determine the total distance traveled and the time required. (Convert cm to m)
