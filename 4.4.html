Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A 5.0 kg object is lifted from the ground to a height of 10 m. First calculate the weight of the object, then determine the work done against gravity, and finally find the potential energy gained. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
2. A 60 kg person climbs from ground level (h = 0) to the top of a building at h = 50 m. Calculate the person's potential energy at ground level, then at the top, and finally determine the change in potential energy. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
3. An engineer lifts a 25 kg toolbox from a truck bed (h = 0.80 m above ground) to a scaffold (h = 5.0 m above ground). Calculate the toolbox's potential energy at the truck, at the scaffold, and find the change in potential energy. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
4. A 2.0 kg book falls from a shelf at height 2.5 m to the floor (h = 0). Calculate its initial potential energy, its final potential energy, and determine how much energy was released. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
5. A 1500 kg elevator starts at ground level and rises to the 10th floor, 30 m high. It then descends to the 5th floor, 15 m high. Calculate potential energy at ground level, at the 10th floor, at the 5th floor, and the net change from start to finish. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
6. A construction worker uses a pulley to lift a 40 kg bag of cement from ground level to a height of 12 m. Calculate the weight of the bag, the work done against gravity, and verify it equals the potential energy gained. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
7. A 0.45 kg soccer ball is kicked upward, reaching a maximum height of 15 m. Calculate its potential energy at ground level, at maximum height, and determine the energy that must have been initially transferred to the ball. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
8. Two objects are at different heights: Object A (3.0 kg at 8.0 m) and Object B (5.0 kg at 4.0 m). Calculate each object's potential energy, then determine which has more and by how much. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
9. A 75 kg mountain climber ascends from base camp (elevation 2000 m) to a summit (elevation 3500 m). Treating the base camp as h = 0, calculate the climber's potential energy at base camp, at the summit, and find the change in potential energy. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
10. A roller coaster car (500 kg) starts at ground level, climbs to a peak at 45 m, then descends to 20 m. Calculate potential energy at each position and find the change from peak to 20 m height. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
11. A crane lifts a 1200 kg steel beam from ground level to a height of 25 m in 40 seconds. Calculate the beam's final potential energy, then determine the average power output by the crane. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$; Power = Work/time)
12. A 12 kg bowling ball is lifted from the floor to a height of 1.8 m. Calculate the potential energy gained. If it is then dropped and reaches the floor with all potential energy converted to kinetic energy, what is the kinetic energy at impact? (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
13. Water (mass 5000 kg) is pumped from a river to a reservoir 80 m higher. Calculate the gravitational potential energy gained by the water. If this process takes 10 minutes, determine the average power required. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
14. A 0.058 kg tennis ball is dropped from a height of 3.0 m. Calculate its initial potential energy. If 20% of this energy is lost to air resistance during the fall, how much kinetic energy will it have just before hitting the ground? (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
15. A 950 kg car drives up a hill, increasing elevation from 100 m to 250 m above sea level. If we use sea level as h = 0, calculate the car's potential energy at both positions, then find the change in potential energy. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
16. A 60 kg person carries a 15 kg backpack up stairs from ground level to a height of 12 m. Calculate the combined potential energy of person plus backpack at the top, and determine the total work done against gravity. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
17. An apple (0.20 kg) falls from a tree branch at h = 4.5 m. Calculate its potential energy at the branch and at ground level (h = 0), then find the decrease in potential energy during the fall. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
18. A 2500 kg truck climbs a mountain road that rises 300 m in elevation. Calculate the increase in gravitational potential energy. If the truck's engine operates at 25% efficiency, how much total energy must the engine produce? (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
19. A pendulum bob (mass 0.50 kg) swings from a height of 0.30 m to a lowest point at 0 m, then back up to 0.25 m. Calculate potential energy at the start, at the lowest point, and at the final position. (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
20. A hydroelectric dam stores water in a reservoir 150 m above the turbines. If 10,000 kg of water flows through per second, calculate the potential energy released per second (which equals power output). (Use $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$)
