Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A 50 kg object is being pushed across a floor by multiple forces. An applied force of 300 N acts to the right while friction exerts 80 N to the left. Calculate the net force, then find the resulting acceleration.
2. During an elevator experiment, a 75 kg person is inside a rising elevator on Earth. First, calculate the person's actual weight using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Then determine the normal force (apparent weight) when the elevator accelerates upward at $2.0 \text{ m/s}^2$.
3. Two horizontal forces are applied to a 60 kg object: 400 N to the right and 150 N to the left. Find the net force. Then determine the acceleration and calculate how long it takes to reach a velocity of 10 m/s starting from rest.
4. A 1200 kg car is pushed by an engine with 5000 N of force while air resistance provides 800 N of opposing force. Calculate the net force, then find the acceleration.
5. A 85 kg box is being pulled across a floor with a rope. The tension in the rope is 400 N horizontally. Friction opposes with 120 N. Calculate the net force, determine the acceleration, and find the distance traveled in 5.0 seconds from rest.
6. A 40 kg student is standing in an elevator that accelerates downward at $1.5 \text{ m/s}^2$. First calculate the student's actual weight on Earth using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Then find the apparent weight (normal force) felt during the downward acceleration.
7. Three horizontal forces act on a 95 kg object: 600 N to the right, 250 N to the left, and 100 N to the left. Calculate the net force, then find the acceleration.
8. A 50 kg box is pulled by a 200 N force horizontally with friction opposing at 80 N. Calculate the net force. Find the acceleration, and determine the velocity after 4.0 seconds of motion from rest.
9. A 1500 kg car needs to accelerate on a highway. The engine provides 6000 N of force and air resistance provides 500 N opposing the motion. Calculate the net force and acceleration. Then find how long it takes to reach a velocity of 25 m/s.
10. A 120 kg object is on a surface where friction exerts 200 N of force. An applied force of 500 N pushes the object. Calculate the net force and acceleration. Then determine how far the object travels in 6.0 seconds starting from rest.
11. Two masses are connected by a rope, with tensions creating forces. For a 40 kg object, a rope tension of 300 N acts on it. Calculate this tension as the net force, then find the object's acceleration.
12. A 70 kg skier is on a slope where the component of weight pulling down the slope is 400 N and friction opposes with 150 N. Calculate the net force down the slope and the acceleration.
13. An elevator with a 90 kg person inside accelerates upward at $3.0 \text{ m/s}^2$. First calculate the person's actual weight using $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. Then determine the apparent weight (normal force). Finally, calculate how much extra force the elevator floor must provide beyond the normal weight.
14. Three forces act on a 200 kg object: 800 N forward, 300 N backward, and 100 N backward. Calculate the net force, then find the acceleration and the distance traveled in 3.0 seconds from rest.
15. A 65 kg box is being dragged with multiple forces: 450 N to the right (applied), 100 N to the left (friction), and 50 N to the left (air resistance). Calculate the net force, find the acceleration, and determine the velocity after 5.0 seconds starting from rest.
