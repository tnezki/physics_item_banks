Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A 0.45 kg soccer ball is kicked from rest to a velocity of 20 m/s. The player's foot is in contact with the ball for 0.025 s. Calculate the change in momentum, then determine the average force exerted by the player's foot.

4. A 75 kg running back moving at 8.5 m/s is tackled and brought to rest in 1.2 s. Determine the running back's initial momentum, calculate the change in momentum, and find the average force exerted during the tackle.

6. A 0.16 kg hockey puck slides at 25 m/s and is struck by a stick, reversing its direction to 30 m/s. The contact lasts 0.040 s. Calculate the change in momentum (being careful with signs), then determine the average force exerted by the stick.
7. A 2200 kg truck traveling east at 18 m/s collides with a barrier and rebounds westward at 6.0 m/s in 1.5 s. Calculate the truck's initial momentum (east positive), its final momentum, the change in momentum, and the average force exerted by the barrier.
8. A driver applies brakes to a 1200 kg car traveling at 28 m/s, bringing it to rest. If the average braking force is 8000 N, first find the impulse needed, then calculate how long the braking takes.
9. A golfer strikes a 0.046 kg golf ball initially at rest. The club exerts an average force of 2400 N for 0.00050 s. Calculate the impulse delivered, then determine the ball's final velocity.
10. A 55 kg person jumps from a platform and lands on the ground, changing velocity from 8.0 m/s downward to zero. If the landing takes 0.20 s, find the change in momentum, then calculate the average force exerted by the ground on the person.
11. A warehouse worker pushes a 85 kg cart from rest to 4.5 m/s in 2.8 s. Calculate the cart's change in momentum, then use the impulse-momentum theorem to find the average force applied.
12. A 0.058 kg tennis ball approaches a racket at 30 m/s and leaves at 40 m/s in the opposite direction. The contact time is 0.0045 s. Calculate the change in momentum, then determine the average force exerted by the racket.
13. A child throws a 0.12 kg ball at a wall at 15 m/s. The ball bounces back at 12 m/s. If contact with the wall lasts 0.080 s, find the ball's change in momentum and the average force exerted by the wall.
14. A safety engineer drops a 70 kg crash test dummy from rest. After falling, the dummy has a velocity of 9.5 m/s downward when it strikes an airbag. The airbag stops the dummy in 0.45 s. Calculate the dummy's momentum just before impact, then find the average force exerted by the airbag.
15. During a rocket launch, exhaust gases with total mass 850 kg are expelled at 1200 m/s in 2.5 s. First calculate the momentum of the expelled gases, then determine the average thrust force produced by the rocket engine.
16. A 3.5 kg sledgehammer moving at 8.0 m/s strikes a stake and comes to rest in 0.015 s. Find the hammer's initial momentum, calculate the impulse required to stop it, and determine the average force on the stake.
17. A defensive player with mass 95 kg running at 7.5 m/s tackles an offensive player, coming to rest together in 0.65 s. Calculate the defensive player's initial momentum, then find the average force exerted during the collision.
18. A force of 550 N acts on a 45 kg object initially at rest for 1.8 s. Calculate the impulse delivered, then use it to find the object's final velocity.
19. A 1800 kg car increases its velocity from 12 m/s to 24 m/s. If the engine provides an average force of 3600 N, first calculate the change in momentum, then determine how long this acceleration takes.
20. A pitcher throws a 0.145 kg baseball horizontally at 38 m/s. A batter hits it back horizontally at 45 m/s. The bat is in contact with the ball for 0.0015 s. Calculate the ball's initial momentum, its final momentum (opposite direction), the change in momentum, and the average force exerted by the bat.
