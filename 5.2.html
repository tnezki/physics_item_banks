Bank 4 – Multi-Step Calculations (Numeric Response)

1. A wave passes a fixed point and 10 complete waves go by in 4.0 seconds. First calculate the frequency, then determine the period of this wave.
2. An ocean wave has wavelength 8.0 m and period 2.0 s. First calculate the frequency, then determine the wave speed.
3. A student observes 15 wave crests pass a dock in 30 seconds. The distance between crests is 6.0 m. Calculate the frequency first, then find the wave speed.
4. A sound wave travels through air at 343 m/s and has a wavelength of 0.686 m. Calculate the frequency, then determine the period.
5. A musician creates a note with frequency 440 Hz. Sound travels at 340 m/s. Calculate the wavelength, then determine how many complete wavelengths exist in a 10 m room.
6. Two waves travel at the same speed of 20 m/s. Wave A has frequency 10 Hz and wave B has frequency 5.0 Hz. Calculate the wavelength of each wave, then determine the ratio of wavelength A to wavelength B.
7. A wave on a rope travels at 8.0 m/s. You observe that 6.0 complete waves fit along a 12 m length of rope. Calculate the wavelength, then find the frequency.
8. A seismograph detects seismic waves arriving with a period of 5.0 s. The waves traveled 20 km. First find the frequency, then calculate the wave speed. (Convert km to m)
9. Light with frequency $5.0 \times 10^{14}$ Hz travels through glass at $2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the wavelength in the glass, then determine what the wavelength would be in vacuum where light travels at $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s.
10. A wave generator produces waves with frequency 4.0 Hz. The waves travel 16 m in 2.0 s. Calculate the wave speed, then determine the wavelength.
11. Sound travels through steel at 5000 m/s. A particular sound has wavelength 10 m in steel. Calculate the frequency in steel. Then determine what the wavelength would be when this same frequency sound travels through air at 340 m/s.
12. An earthquake produces P-waves (longitudinal) traveling at 6000 m/s and S-waves (transverse) at 3500 m/s. For waves with frequency 2.0 Hz, calculate the wavelength of each type.
13. A water wave has wavelength 3.0 m. You count 20 waves passing a buoy in 40 s. Calculate the frequency, then determine the wave speed.
14. Radio station broadcasts at 100 MHz (megahertz = $10^6$ Hz). Radio waves travel at $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the wavelength, then determine the period.
15. Two waves travel in the same medium at speed 24 m/s. Wave 1 has period 0.40 s and wave 2 has period 0.80 s. Calculate the frequency of each wave, then find the wavelength of each.
16. A wave machine produces 5.0 waves per second with wavelength 0.60 m. Calculate the wave speed. Then determine how long it takes one wave to travel 15 m.
17. An ultrasonic wave used in medical imaging has frequency 3.0 MHz ($3.0 \times 10^6$ Hz) and travels through tissue at 1500 m/s. Calculate the wavelength, then determine how many complete wavelengths fit in a 3.0 cm tissue depth. (Convert cm to m)
18. A tsunami in the open ocean has wavelength 150 km and period 30 minutes. Convert the period to seconds, then calculate the frequency and wave speed.
19. A wave pool generates waves with frequency 0.50 Hz. The waves reflect off the far wall 25 m away and return in 10 s total travel time. Calculate the wave speed, then determine the wavelength.
20. A guitar string vibrates at 330 Hz. The string is 0.65 m long and has 3 complete wavelengths along its length. Calculate the wavelength first, then determine the wave speed on the string.
